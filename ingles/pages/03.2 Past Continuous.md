
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** The Past Continuous is used for actions that were in progress at a specific moment in the past.
>
> **Español:** El Pasado Continuo se usa para acciones que estaban en progreso en un momento específico del pasado.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I was studying at 8 p.m.
> - She was cooking dinner.
> - They were playing soccer.
> - We were watching a movie.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + was/were + Verb-ing + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I was studying English. | Estaba estudiando inglés. |
| She was reading a book. | Ella estaba leyendo un libro. |
| We were watching TV. | Estábamos viendo televisión. |
| They were playing soccer. | Ellos estaban jugando fútbol. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + was/were + not + Verb-ing**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I was not sleeping. | No estaba durmiendo. |
| She was not working. | Ella no estaba trabajando. |
| They were not listening. | Ellos no estaban escuchando. |

### Contractions

| Full Form | Short Form |
|------------|------------|
| I was not | I wasn't |
| He was not | He wasn't |
| She was not | She wasn't |
| We were not | We weren't |
| They were not | They weren't |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Was/Were + Subject + Verb-ing?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Were you studying? | ¿Estabas estudiando? |
| Was she cooking? | ¿Ella estaba cocinando? |
| Were they playing? | ¿Ellos estaban jugando? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I was. | Sí. |
| No, I wasn't. | No. |
| Yes, she was. | Sí. |
| No, they weren't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of the Past Continuous

## 1. Actions in Progress at a Specific Time

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I was studying at 9 p.m. | Estaba estudiando a las 9 p.m. |
| They were working this morning. | Estaban trabajando esta mañana. |

---

## 2. Interrupted Actions

A longer action was interrupted by a shorter action.

Una acción larga fue interrumpida por una acción corta.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I was sleeping when you called. | Estaba durmiendo cuando llamaste. |
| She was cooking when the lights went out. | Ella estaba cocinando cuando se fue la luz. |

---

## 3. Two Actions Happening at the Same Time

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I was studying while my brother was watching TV. | Estaba estudiando mientras mi hermano veía televisión. |
| They were talking while we were eating. | Ellos conversaban mientras nosotros comíamos. |

---

## 4. Background Descriptions

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| The birds were singing, and the sun was shining. | Los pájaros estaban cantando y el sol estaba brillando. |
| People were walking in the park. | La gente estaba caminando en el parque. |

---

# 🔑 Signal Words / Palabras Clave

Common expressions used with the Past Continuous:

- while
- when
- at that moment
- all day
- all night
- at 8 p.m.
- yesterday at this time

### Examples

- She was sleeping when I arrived.
- We were studying while they were playing.
- I was working at that moment.

---

# ⚖️ Past Continuous vs Simple Past

| Past Continuous | Simple Past |
|-----------------|-------------|
| I was reading when she arrived. | She arrived at 8 p.m. |
| They were playing while it was raining. | It rained yesterday. |
| Action in progress. | Completed action. |

---

# 📍 WAS and WERE

## Use WAS with:

- I
- He
- She
- It

### Examples

- I was tired.
- She was studying.

---

## Use WERE with:

- You
- We
- They

### Examples

- You were late.
- They were playing.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I was study English. | I was studying English. |
>| They was playing. | They were playing. |
>| Was you sleeping? | Were you sleeping? |
>| She were working. | She was working. |
>| We weren't play soccer. | We weren't playing soccer. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - The Past Continuous always uses **was/were + verb-ing**.
> - The main verb must end in **-ing**.
> - Use **was** and **were** with the correct subjects.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Michael: What were you doing yesterday at 8 p.m.?

Emily: I was studying for my history exam.

Michael: Really? I called you, but you didn't answer.

Emily: Sorry! I was reading a chapter, and my phone was in another room.

Michael: No problem. I was helping my brother with his homework.

Emily: Were you working on math?

Michael: Yes, we were solving some difficult exercises.

Emily: While you were doing that, I was preparing for my test.

Michael: It sounds like we were both very busy.

---

## Español

Michael: ¿Qué estabas haciendo ayer a las 8 p.m.?

Emily: Estaba estudiando para mi examen de historia.

Michael: ¿En serio? Te llamé, pero no contestaste.

Emily: ¡Lo siento! Estaba leyendo un capítulo y mi teléfono estaba en otra habitación.

Michael: No hay problema. Yo estaba ayudando a mi hermano con su tarea.

Emily: ¿Estaban trabajando matemáticas?

Michael: Sí, estábamos resolviendo algunos ejercicios difíciles.

Emily: Mientras hacían eso, yo estaba preparándome para mi examen.

Michael: Parece que ambos estábamos muy ocupados.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- Actions in progress in the past
- Interrupted actions
- Two simultaneous actions
- Background descriptions

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + was/were + Verb-ing

### Negative

Subject + was/were + not + Verb-ing

### Question

Was/Were + Subject + Verb-ing?

## Signal Words

- while
- when
- at that moment
- all day
- yesterday at this time

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **was/were + verb-ing**.
> - The action was in progress in the past.
> - Use **while** for simultaneous actions.
> - Use **when** for interrupted actions.
> - Learn the difference between the Past Continuous and the Simple Past.
> - Always use the **-ing** form of the main verb.
