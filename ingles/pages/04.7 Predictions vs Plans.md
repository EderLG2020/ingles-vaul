
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** In English, we use different structures for **predictions** and **plans**.
>
> - **Will** → predictions and spontaneous decisions.
> - **Going to** → plans and predictions with evidence.
> - **Present Continuous** → fixed personal arrangements.
> - **Present Simple** → schedules and timetables.
>
> **Español:** En inglés usamos diferentes estructuras para **predicciones** y **planes**.
>
> - **Will** → predicciones y decisiones espontáneas.
> - **Going to** → planes y predicciones con evidencia.
> - **Present Continuous** → arreglos personales definidos.
> - **Present Simple** → horarios e itinerarios.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I think it will rain.
> - I am going to study tonight.
> - We are meeting our teacher tomorrow.
> - The train leaves at 8 a.m.

# 🔮 What Is a Prediction? / ¿Qué Es una Predicción?

A prediction is something we believe will happen in the future.

Una predicción es algo que creemos que sucederá en el futuro.

---

## Using Will

### Formula

**Subject + will + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I think it will rain. | Creo que lloverá. |
| She will pass the exam. | Ella aprobará el examen. |
| People will live longer. | La gente vivirá más tiempo. |

### Common Expressions

- I think...
- I believe...
- Maybe...
- Probably...
- I'm sure...
- I guess...

---

## Using Going To for Predictions

Use **going to** when there is evidence.

Usa **going to** cuando existe una evidencia.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Look at those clouds! It is going to rain. | ¡Mira esas nubes! Va a llover. |
| Be careful! You are going to fall. | ¡Ten cuidado! Te vas a caer. |

---

# 📅 What Is a Plan? / ¿Qué Es un Plan?

A plan is something decided before the moment of speaking.

Un plan es algo decidido antes del momento de hablar.

---

## Using Going To

### Formula

**Subject + am/is/are + going to + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I am going to study medicine. | Voy a estudiar medicina. |
| We are going to travel next month. | Vamos a viajar el próximo mes. |
| She is going to buy a new computer. | Ella va a comprar una nueva computadora. |

---

## Using Present Continuous

Use the Present Continuous for arrangements that are already organized.

Usa el Presente Continuo para planes que ya están organizados.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I am meeting my friends tonight. | Me reuniré con mis amigos esta noche. |
| They are flying to Chile tomorrow. | Ellos viajarán a Chile mañana. |

---

## Using Present Simple

Use the Present Simple for schedules and timetables.

Usa el Presente Simple para horarios e itinerarios.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| The train leaves at 7 a.m. | El tren sale a las 7 a.m. |
| The class starts tomorrow. | La clase comienza mañana. |

---

# ⚖️ Future Structures Comparison

| Structure | Main Use | Example |
|------------|----------|----------|
| Will | Prediction | I think she will win. |
| Going To | Plan | I am going to study tonight. |
| Going To | Prediction with evidence | It is going to rain. |
| Present Continuous | Fixed arrangement | I am meeting my teacher tomorrow. |
| Present Simple | Schedule | The bus leaves at 6 p.m. |

---

# 📍 Decision at the Moment vs Previous Plan

| Situation | Correct Structure |
|------------|------------------|
| Instant decision | Will |
| Planned action | Going to |

### Examples

**Situation:** The phone is ringing.

✅ I will answer it.

---

**Situation:** You decided yesterday.

✅ I am going to answer all my emails tonight.

---

# 📍 Prediction Without Evidence vs With Evidence

| Situation | Correct Structure |
|------------|------------------|
| Opinion | Will |
| Visible evidence | Going to |

### Examples

- I think our team will win.
- Look at the score! Our team is going to win.

---

# 📍 Personal Arrangement vs Public Schedule

| Personal Arrangement | Public Schedule |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| I am meeting my doctor tomorrow. | The clinic opens at 8 a.m. |
| We are having dinner tonight. | The movie starts at 7 p.m. |

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I think it is going to rain. (without evidence) | I think it will rain. |
>| I will study medicine next year. (already planned) | I am going to study medicine next year. |
>| I am meeting the train at 8 a.m. | The train leaves at 8 a.m. |
>| I will meet my dentist tomorrow. (appointment already arranged) | I am meeting my dentist tomorrow. |
>| Look at the clouds! It will rain. | Look at the clouds! It is going to rain. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - **Will** is common for opinions and predictions.
> - **Going to** expresses plans and evidence-based predictions.
> - **Present Continuous** expresses arranged personal plans.
> - **Present Simple** expresses official schedules.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Emily: What are your plans for next weekend?

Daniel: I am going to visit my grandparents.

Emily: Nice! I am meeting some friends on Saturday.

Daniel: That sounds fun. What time are you meeting them?

Emily: We are meeting at six in the evening.

Daniel: Great. I think the weather will be nice.

Emily: I hope so, but look at those clouds. It is going to rain today.

Daniel: Maybe, but according to the forecast, the weather will improve tomorrow.

Emily: Perfect! Then we will have a great weekend.

---

## Español

Emily: ¿Cuáles son tus planes para el próximo fin de semana?

Daniel: Voy a visitar a mis abuelos.

Emily: ¡Qué bien! Yo me reuniré con unos amigos el sábado.

Daniel: Suena divertido. ¿A qué hora se reunirán?

Emily: Nos reuniremos a las seis de la tarde.

Daniel: Genial. Creo que el clima será agradable.

Emily: Eso espero, pero mira esas nubes. Va a llover hoy.

Daniel: Tal vez, pero según el pronóstico, el clima mejorará mañana.

Emily: ¡Perfecto! Entonces tendremos un gran fin de semana.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Future Structures

| Structure | Use |
|------------|------|
| Will | Predictions and spontaneous decisions |
| Going To | Plans and evidence-based predictions |
| Present Continuous | Fixed arrangements |
| Present Simple | Schedules and timetables |

## Quick Guide

### Prediction (Opinion)

- I think it will rain.

### Prediction (Evidence)

- Look! It is going to rain.

### Plan

- I am going to study tonight.

### Arrangement

- I am meeting my teacher tomorrow.

### Schedule

- The class starts at 8 a.m.

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **will** for opinions and instant decisions.
> - Use **going to** for plans and visible evidence.
> - Use the **Present Continuous** for organized arrangements.
> - Use the **Present Simple** for timetables and schedules.
> - Choose the future form according to the situation, not only the time.
> - Understanding the difference between predictions and plans makes your English sound much more natural.
