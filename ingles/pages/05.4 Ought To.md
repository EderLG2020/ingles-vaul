
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** **Ought to** is a modal expression used to give **advice, recommendations, moral obligations, and expectations**. It has a meaning very similar to **should**, but it is slightly more formal.
>
> **Español:** **Ought to** es una expresión modal que se usa para dar **consejos, recomendaciones, obligaciones morales y expectativas**. Su significado es muy parecido a **should**, pero es un poco más formal.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - You ought to study more.
> - We ought to help others.
> - She ought to see a doctor.
> - They ought to arrive soon.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + ought to + Base Verb + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| You ought to study every day. | Deberías estudiar todos los días. |
| She ought to apologize. | Ella debería disculparse. |
| We ought to protect nature. | Deberíamos proteger la naturaleza. |
| They ought to save money. | Ellos deberían ahorrar dinero. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + ought not to + Base Verb**

### Contraction

Although **oughtn't to** exists, **ought not to** is much more common.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| You ought not to lie. | No deberías mentir. |
| She ought not to worry so much. | Ella no debería preocuparse tanto. |
| They ought not to waste food. | Ellos no deberían desperdiciar comida. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

Questions with **ought to** are possible but uncommon in modern English. Native speakers usually prefer **should**.

Las preguntas con **ought to** son posibles, pero poco comunes en el inglés moderno. Los hablantes nativos suelen preferir **should**.

### Formula / Fórmula

**Ought + Subject + to + Base Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Ought I to call her? | ¿Debería llamarla? |
| Ought we to wait? | ¿Deberíamos esperar? |

### More Natural Alternatives

| Natural English | Español |
|-----------------|----------|
| Should I call her? | ¿Debería llamarla? |
| Should we wait? | ¿Deberíamos esperar? |

Talking about what is morally right.

# ⚖️ Ought To vs Should

| Ought To | Should |
|-----------|---------|
| Slightly more formal | More common |
| Strong moral advice | General advice |
| You ought to help. | You should help. |
| We ought to be honest. | We should be honest. |

---

# ⚖️ Ought To vs Must

| Ought To | Must |
|-----------|-------|
| Advice or moral duty | Strong obligation |
| You ought to respect others. | You must wear a helmet. |
| Recommendation | Requirement |

---

# 📍 Rules for Ought To

After **ought to**, always use the base form of the verb.

Después de **ought to**, siempre se usa el verbo en su forma base.

### Correct

- You ought to study.
- She ought to wait.
- They ought to leave.

### Incorrect

- You ought to studying.
- She ought to waits.
- They ought to to leave.

---

# 📍 Ought To Have

**Ought to have + Past Participle** is used for past advice, criticism, or regret.

**Ought to have + Participio Pasado** se usa para expresar consejos pasados, críticas o arrepentimiento.

### Examples

- You ought to have called me.
- I ought to have studied harder.
- They ought to have arrived earlier.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| You ought study more. | You ought to study more. |
>| She ought to goes home. | She ought to go home. |
>| Ought I call him? | Ought I to call him? |
>| They ought to studying harder. | They ought to study harder. |
>| He oughts to work more. | He ought to work more. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - **Ought** is almost always followed by **to**.
> - After **ought to**, use the base form of the verb.
> - Do not add **-s**, **-ed**, or **-ing** to the main verb.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Nathan: I failed my English test.

Olivia: I'm sorry to hear that. You ought to study a little every day.

Nathan: I know. I also spend too much time on my phone.

Olivia: You ought not to use your phone while studying.

Nathan: You're right. What else ought I to do?

Olivia: You ought to practice speaking and reading more often.

Nathan: That sounds like good advice.

Olivia: Don't worry. If you work hard, you ought to improve quickly.

Nathan: Thanks! I ought to start today.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- Advice
- Recommendations
- Moral obligations
- Expectations

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + ought to + Base Verb

### Negative

Subject + ought not to + Base Verb

### Question

Ought + Subject + to + Base Verb?

## Common Expressions

- You ought to...
- We ought to...
- Ought I to...?
- Ought not to...

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **ought to** for advice and moral duty.
> - It is very similar to **should**, but slightly more formal.
> - Always use **to** after **ought**.
> - After **ought to**, use the base form of the verb.
> - Use **ought to have + past participle** for past advice or regret.
> - In everyday conversation, **should** is often more common than **ought to**.
