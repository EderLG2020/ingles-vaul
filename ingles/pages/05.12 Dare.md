

> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** **Dare** can be used as a **main verb** or as a **modal verb**. It is used to talk about **courage, challenge, or having the confidence to do something**.
>
> **Español:** **Dare** puede usarse como **verbo principal** o como **verbo modal**. Se utiliza para hablar sobre **valor, desafío o tener el coraje de hacer algo**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I dare to speak English.
> - She doesn't dare to ask.
> - Dare you tell him the truth?
> - Nobody dared say anything.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## 1. Dare as a Main Verb

This is the most common use in modern English.

Este es el uso más común en el inglés moderno.

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + dare/dared + to + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I dare to speak in public. | Me atrevo a hablar en público. |
| She dares to ask difficult questions. | Ella se atreve a hacer preguntas difíciles. |
| They dared to climb the mountain. | Se atrevieron a escalar la montaña. |

---

## 2. Dare as a Modal Verb

This use is more formal and less common.

Este uso es más formal y menos común.

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + dare + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Dare you tell him? | ¿Te atreves a decírselo? |
| Nobody dare speak. | Nadie se atreve a hablar. |
| How dare you say that! | ¡Cómo te atreves a decir eso! |

---

# 🏗️ Negative Forms

## Main Verb

### Formula

**Subject + do/does not + dare to + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I don't dare to ask. | No me atrevo a preguntar. |
| She doesn't dare to drive at night. | Ella no se atreve a conducir de noche. |

---

## Modal Verb

### Formula

**Subject + dare not + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I dare not tell him. | No me atrevo a decírselo. |
| They dare not complain. | No se atreven a quejarse. |

| English | Español |

# 📍 Rules for Dare

## Main Verb

Use **to** before the main verb.

### Examples

- I dare to try.
- She dares to ask.

## Modal Verb

Do not use **to**.

### Examples

- Dare you speak?
- I dare not move.

---

# 📍 Dared To (Past)

### Examples

- I dared to tell the truth.
- She dared to leave her job.
- They dared to dream big.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I dare speak English. (common context) | I dare to speak English. |
>| She dare to ask. | She dares to ask. |
>| I don't dare ask him. | I don't dare to ask him. |
>| Dare you to come? | Dare you come? |
>| He dares not to speak. | He dare not speak. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - The **main verb** form normally uses **to**.
> - The **modal verb** form does not use **to**.
> - With **he, she, it**, use **dares** when it is a main verb.
> - Modal verbs do not change form.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Lucas: Why don't you join the singing contest?

Maya: Because I don't dare to sing in front of people.

Lucas: Really? I think you have a beautiful voice.

Maya: Thanks, but I'm still nervous.

Lucas: I dare you to try. You might enjoy it.

Maya: Do you really think I can do it?

Lucas: Of course. You have practiced a lot.

Maya: Then I will do it. I want to be more confident.

Lucas: That's the spirit! Sometimes we have to dare to follow our dreams.

---

## Español

Lucas: ¿Por qué no participas en el concurso de canto?

Maya: Porque no me atrevo a cantar frente a la gente.

Lucas: ¿En serio? Creo que tienes una hermosa voz.

Maya: Gracias, pero todavía estoy nerviosa.

Lucas: Te reto a intentarlo. Puede que te guste.

Maya: ¿De verdad crees que puedo hacerlo?

Lucas: Claro. Has practicado mucho.

Maya: Entonces lo haré. Quiero tener más confianza.

Lucas: ¡Esa es la actitud! A veces tenemos que atrevernos a seguir nuestros sueños.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- Courage
- Challenges
- Fear or hesitation
- Surprise and anger

## Basic Structures

### Main Verb

Subject + dare to + Base Verb

### Modal Verb

Subject + dare + Base Verb

### Negative

- don't/doesn't dare to...
- dare not...

## Common Expressions

- I dare to...
- I don't dare to...
- Dare you...?
- How dare you...!
- I dare you to...

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - **Dare** can function as both a main verb and a modal verb.
> - The main verb usually uses **to**.
> - The modal verb does not use **to**.
> - **How dare you...!** is a very common expression of anger or surprise.
> - **I dare you to...** is commonly used to challenge someone.
> - In modern English, the main verb form is much more common than the modal form.
