
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** The Simple Present is used for habits, routines, facts, and general truths.
>
> **Español:** El Presente Simple se usa para hábitos, rutinas, hechos y verdades generales.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I study English every day.
> - She works in a hospital.
> - The sun rises in the east.
> - They play soccer on weekends.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + Verb + Object**

For **he, she, it**, add **-s** or **-es** to the verb.

Para **he, she, it**, se agrega **-s** o **-es** al verbo.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I work. | Yo trabajo. |
| You study English. | Tú estudias inglés. |
| He plays soccer. | Él juega fútbol. |
| She reads books. | Ella lee libros. |
| It rains a lot. | Llueve mucho. |
| We live in Peru. | Nosotros vivimos en Perú. |
| They watch TV. | Ellos ven televisión. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + do not (don't) + Verb**

**Subject + does not (doesn't) + Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I don't work on Sundays. | No trabajo los domingos. |
| You don't like coffee. | No te gusta el café. |
| He doesn't play tennis. | Él no juega tenis. |
| She doesn't eat meat. | Ella no come carne. |
| We don't watch TV every day. | No vemos televisión todos los días. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Do + Subject + Verb?**

**Does + Subject + Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Do you speak English? | ¿Hablas inglés? |
| Do they work here? | ¿Trabajan aquí? |
| Does he play soccer? | ¿Él juega fútbol? |
| Does she study medicine? | ¿Ella estudia medicina? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I do. | Sí. |
| No, I don't. | No. |
| Yes, he does. | Sí. |
| No, she doesn't. | No. |

# 👤 Third Person Singular (He / She / It)

For the third person singular, most verbs add **-s**.

Para la tercera persona singular, la mayoría de los verbos agregan **-s**.

| Base Verb | Third Person |
|------------|--------------|
| work | works |
| play | plays |
| read | reads |
| drink | drinks |
| eat | eats |

## Verbs ending in -sh, -ch, -x, -s, -o

Add **-es**.

| Base Verb | Third Person |
|------------|--------------|
| watch | watches |
| wash | washes |
| go | goes |
| fix | fixes |
| pass | passes |

## Verbs ending in consonant + y

Change **y → ies**

| Base Verb | Third Person |
|------------|--------------|
| study | studies |
| carry | carries |
| try | tries |

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| He play soccer. | He plays soccer. |
>| She don't like coffee. | She doesn't like coffee. |
>| Does he plays soccer? | Does he play soccer? |
>| I am work every day. | I work every day. |
>| He study English. | He studies English. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - The third person singular requires **-s** or **-es**.
> - After **does**, the main verb returns to its base form.
> - The Simple Present does not use **to be** before ordinary verbs.

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use the Simple Present for habits and facts.
> - Add **-s/-es** for **he, she, it**.
> - Use **don't** and **doesn't** for negatives.
> - Use **do** and **does** for questions.
> - After **does**, use the base form of the verb.
> - Learn common frequency adverbs to sound natural.
