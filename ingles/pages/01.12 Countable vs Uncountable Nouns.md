
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** Countable nouns can be counted and have singular and plural forms. Uncountable nouns cannot normally be counted individually.
>
> **Español:** Los sustantivos contables se pueden contar y tienen forma singular y plural. Los sustantivos incontables normalmente no se pueden contar de forma individual.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - One apple → Two apples.
> - One book → Three books.
> - Water ❌ (not *two waters* in general use)
> - Milk ❌ (not *three milks* in general use)

# 🔢 Countable Nouns / Sustantivos Contables

Countable nouns are things we can count.

Los sustantivos contables son cosas que podemos contar.

## Examples

| Singular | Plural | Español |
|----------|----------|----------|
| a book | books | libro(s) |
| an apple | apples | manzana(s) |
| a car | cars | auto(s) |
| a student | students | estudiante(s) |
| a chair | chairs | silla(s) |

### Example Sentences

- I have a book.
- She has three apples.
- They bought two cars.

---

# 🌊 Uncountable Nouns / Sustantivos Incontables

Uncountable nouns cannot usually be counted individually.

Los sustantivos incontables normalmente no se pueden contar de manera individual.

## Common Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| water | agua |
| milk | leche |
| rice | arroz |
| sugar | azúcar |
| money | dinero |
| information | información |
| advice | consejo |
| furniture | muebles |
| music | música |
| bread | pan |

### Example Sentences

- I drink water.
- She likes music.
- We need more information.

# 🍎 Quantifiers / Cuantificadores

## For Countable Nouns

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| a / an | un / una |
| one | uno |
| two | dos |
| many | muchos |
| a few | algunos |

### Examples

- I have two books.
- She has many friends.
- We bought a few apples.

---

## For Uncountable Nouns

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| some | algo de |
| much | mucho |
| a little | un poco de |
| a lot of | mucho(s) |
| little | poco |

### Examples

- I need some water.
- She has a little money.
- We have a lot of rice.

---

# 📦 Containers for Uncountable Nouns

We often use containers or units to count uncountable nouns.

A menudo usamos recipientes o unidades para contar sustantivos incontables.

| Expression | Español |
|------------|----------|
| a glass of water | un vaso de agua |
| a bottle of milk | una botella de leche |
| a cup of coffee | una taza de café |
| a loaf of bread | una barra de pan |
| a piece of information | una información |
| a piece of advice | un consejo |

### Examples

- Can I have a glass of water?
- She bought a loaf of bread.
- He gave me a piece of advice.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| an information | some information |
>| two breads | two loaves of bread |
>| many money | much money |
>| an advice | some advice |
>| many furniture | much furniture |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - Some English nouns are uncountable even if they are countable in Spanish.
> - Information, advice, furniture, money, and bread are usually uncountable.

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Countable nouns have singular and plural forms.
> - Uncountable nouns usually do not have a plural form.
> - Use **many** with countable nouns.
> - Use **much** with uncountable nouns.
> - Use containers like **a glass of**, **a bottle of**, or **a piece of** with uncountable nouns.
> - Some nouns are uncountable in English even if they are countable in Spanish.
