
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** We use **be going to** to talk about future plans, intentions, and predictions based on present evidence.
>
> **Español:** Usamos **be going to** para hablar sobre planes, intenciones y predicciones basadas en evidencias presentes.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I am going to study tonight.
> - She is going to travel next month.
> - Look at those clouds! It is going to rain.
> - They are going to buy a new house.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + am/is/are + going to + Base Verb + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I am going to study. | Voy a estudiar. |
| She is going to work tomorrow. | Ella va a trabajar mañana. |
| They are going to travel. | Ellos van a viajar. |
| We are going to watch a movie. | Vamos a ver una película. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + am/is/are + not + going to + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I am not going to stay home. | No voy a quedarme en casa. |
| She is not going to drive today. | Ella no va a conducir hoy. |
| They are not going to sell the car. | Ellos no van a vender el auto. |

### Contractions

| Full Form | Short Form |
|------------|------------|
| I am | I'm |
| He is | He's |
| She is | She's |
| We are | We're |
| They are | They're |
| is not | isn't |
| are not | aren't |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Am/Is/Are + Subject + going to + Base Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Are you going to study? | ¿Vas a estudiar? |
| Is she going to come? | ¿Ella va a venir? |
| Are they going to travel? | ¿Ellos van a viajar? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I am. | Sí. |
| No, I'm not. | No. |
| Yes, she is. | Sí. |
| No, they aren't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of Going To

## 1. Future Plans

Plans made before the moment of speaking.

Planes hechos antes del momento de hablar.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I am going to study medicine. | Voy a estudiar medicina. |
| We are going to visit our grandparents. | Vamos a visitar a nuestros abuelos. |

---

## 2. Intentions

Things we intend to do.

Cosas que tenemos la intención de hacer.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| She is going to learn French. | Ella va a aprender francés. |
| They are going to start a business. | Ellos van a iniciar un negocio. |

---

## 3. Predictions Based on Evidence

Predictions supported by what we can see.

Predicciones basadas en lo que podemos observar.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Look at the sky! It is going to rain. | ¡Mira el cielo! Va a llover. |
| Be careful! You are going to fall. | ¡Ten cuidado! Te vas a caer. |

---

# 🔑 Signal Words / Palabras Clave

Common expressions used with **going to**:

- tomorrow
- tonight
- next week
- next month
- next year
- this weekend
- soon
- in the future

### Examples

- I am going to visit my cousin tomorrow.
- She is going to move next year.
- We are going to play soccer this weekend.

---

# ⚖️ Going To vs Will

| Going To | Will |
|-----------|------|
| Planned action | Spontaneous decision |
| Prior intention | Instant decision |
| Prediction with evidence | General prediction |
| I am going to study tonight. | I will answer the phone. |

### Examples

- I am going to buy a new laptop.
- I will help you with that.

---

# 📍 Am / Is / Are

## Use AM with

- I

### Example

- I am going to travel.

---

## Use IS with

- He
- She
- It

### Examples

- He is going to cook.
- She is going to study.

---

## Use ARE with

- You
- We
- They

### Examples

- We are going to leave.
- They are going to play.

---

# 📍 Going To Be

Use **going to be** for future situations or states.

### Examples

- It is going to be a great day.
- She is going to be a doctor.
- They are going to be late.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I going to study. | I am going to study. |
>| She is going to studies. | She is going to study. |
>| Are you going study? | Are you going to study? |
>| They are going to playing. | They are going to play. |
>| He going to travel. | He is going to travel. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - Always use **am / is / are** before **going to**.
> - Always include **to**.
> - After **going to**, use the base form of the verb.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Mark: What are you going to do this weekend?

Julia: I am going to visit my grandparents.

Mark: That sounds nice. Are you going to stay there long?

Julia: Yes, I am going to stay until Sunday.

Mark: I am going to finish my science project.

Julia: Good luck! Are you going to work alone?

Mark: No, my friends are going to help me.

Julia: Great! After that, we are going to watch a movie.

Mark: That sounds like a perfect weekend.

Mark: ¿Qué vas a hacer este fin de semana?

Julia: Voy a visitar a mis abuelos.

Mark: Suena bien. ¿Vas a quedarte mucho tiempo?

Julia: Sí, voy a quedarme hasta el domingo.

Mark: Yo voy a terminar mi proyecto de ciencias.

Julia: ¡Buena suerte! ¿Vas a trabajar solo?

Mark: No, mis amigos van a ayudarme.

Julia: ¡Genial! Después vamos a ver una película.

Mark: Suena como un fin de semana perfecto.

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **going to** for plans already decided.
> - Use **going to** for intentions.
> - Use **going to** for predictions with evidence.

> - After **going to**, always use the base form of the verb.
