

> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** **Modal Perfects** are formed with **Modal Verb + have + Past Participle**. They are used to talk about **past possibilities, obligations, regrets, deductions, and hypothetical situations**.
>
> **Español:** Los **Modal Perfects** se forman con **Verbo Modal + have + Participio Pasado**. Se usan para hablar sobre **posibilidades pasadas, obligaciones, arrepentimientos, deducciones y situaciones hipotéticas**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - She must have forgotten.
> - You should have studied.
> - They might have missed the bus.
> - I could have helped you.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Formula / Fórmula

**Modal Verb + have + Past Participle**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| She must have left. | Ella debe haberse ido. |
| You should have called me. | Deberías haberme llamado. |
| I could have won. | Podría haber ganado. |
| They might have forgotten. | Puede que hayan olvidado. |

---

# 📅 Types of Modal Perfects

## 1. Must Have

### Meaning

Strong logical deduction about the past.

Conclusión lógica fuerte sobre el pasado.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| She isn't here. She must have left. | Ella no está aquí. Debe haberse ido. |
| They look tired. They must have worked hard. | Se ven cansados. Deben haber trabajado duro. |

---

## 2. Should Have

### Meaning

Past advice, criticism, or regret.

Consejo, crítica o arrepentimiento sobre el pasado.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| You should have studied. | Deberías haber estudiado. |
| I should have listened to you. | Debería haberte escuchado. |

---

## 3. Could Have

### Meaning

Past possibility or missed opportunity.

Posibilidad pasada u oportunidad perdida.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I could have helped you. | Podría haberte ayudado. |
| They could have won the game. | Podrían haber ganado el partido. |

---

## 4. Might Have

### Meaning

Uncertain possibility about the past.

Posibilidad incierta sobre el pasado.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| She might have forgotten. | Puede que haya olvidado. |
| We might have taken the wrong bus. | Puede que hayamos tomado el autobús equivocado. |

---

## 5. May Have

### Meaning

Possible past event.

Posible evento pasado.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| He may have arrived already. | Puede que ya haya llegado. |
| They may have missed the meeting. | Puede que hayan perdido la reunión. |

---

## 6. Would Have

### Meaning

Hypothetical past situations.

Situaciones hipotéticas en el pasado.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I would have gone with you. | Habría ido contigo. |
| She would have accepted the offer. | Ella habría aceptado la oferta. |

---

## 7. Ought To Have

### Meaning

Past moral obligation or regret.

Obligación moral o arrepentimiento pasado.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| You ought to have apologized. | Deberías haberte disculpado. |
| We ought to have left earlier. | Deberíamos haber salido antes. |

---

# ⚖️ Must Have vs Might Have

| Must Have | Might Have |
|------------|-------------|
| Strong certainty | Uncertain possibility |
| She must have left. | She might have left. |
| Very likely | Maybe |

---

# ⚖️ Should Have vs Could Have

| Should Have | Could Have |
|--------------|-------------|
| Regret or criticism | Possibility or ability |
| You should have called. | You could have called. |

---

# ⚖️ Would Have vs Could Have

| Would Have | Could Have |
|-------------|-------------|
| Hypothetical result | Possible action |
| I would have gone. | I could have gone. |

---

# 📍 Rules for Modal Perfects

Always follow this order:

**Modal + have + Past Participle**

### Correct

- She must have left.
- I should have studied.
- They could have helped.

### Incorrect

- She must has left.
- I should studied.
- They could have helping.

---

# 📍 Past Participles

Remember that regular verbs end in **-ed**, but irregular verbs have special forms.

| Base Verb | Past Participle |
|------------|----------------|
| work | worked |
| study | studied |
| go | gone |
| eat | eaten |
| see | seen |
| write | written |
| take | taken |
| do | done |

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| She must has left. | She must have left. |
>| I should studied more. | I should have studied more. |
>| They could have went. | They could have gone. |
>| He might has forgotten. | He might have forgotten. |
>| We would have helping you. | We would have helped you. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - Always use **have** after the modal.
> - Always use the **past participle**.
> - Never use the simple past after **have**.
> - Never change the modal verb.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Emily: Jack isn't answering his phone.

Oliver: He must have left work already.

Emily: Maybe. He might have forgotten his phone too.

Oliver: That's possible. He should have told us.

Emily: Yes, and we could have waited for him.

Oliver: If he had called, we would have picked him up.

Emily: You're right. He ought to have sent a message.

Oliver: Don't worry. He may have been very busy.

Emily: I hope everything is fine.

---

## Español

Emily: Jack no responde su teléfono.

Oliver: Debe haberse ido del trabajo.

Emily: Tal vez. Puede que también haya olvidado su teléfono.

Oliver: Es posible. Debería habernos avisado.

Emily: Sí, y podríamos haberlo esperado.

Oliver: Si hubiera llamado, lo habríamos recogido.

Emily: Tienes razón. Debería haber enviado un mensaje.

Oliver: No te preocupes. Puede que haya estado muy ocupado.

Emily: Espero que todo esté bien.

## Formula

**Modal + have + Past Participle**

## Main Uses

| Structure | Use |
|------------|------|
| Must have | Logical deduction |
| Should have | Regret or criticism |
| Could have | Missed opportunity |
| Might have | Uncertain possibility |
| May have | Possible event |
| Would have | Hypothetical situation |
| Ought to have | Moral obligation or regret |

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Always use **Modal + have + Past Participle**.
> - Never use the simple past after **have**.
> - Use **must have** for strong deductions.
> - Use **should have** for regrets and criticism.
> - Use **could have** for missed opportunities.
> - Use **might have** and **may have** for possibilities.
> - Use **would have** for hypothetical past situations.
> - Use **ought to have** for moral obligations or regrets.
