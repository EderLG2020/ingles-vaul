

> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** **Will** is a modal verb used to express **future predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises, offers, and future facts**.
>
> **Español:** **Will** es un verbo modal que se usa para expresar **predicciones futuras, decisiones espontáneas, promesas, ofrecimientos y hechos futuros**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I will help you.
> - She will arrive tomorrow.
> - I think it will rain.
> - Will you marry me?
>
> **Note:** As a modal verb, **will** is different from the future tense topic studied in Unit 4. Here, the focus is on its modal uses.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + will + Base Verb + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I will help you. | Te ayudaré. |
| She will come tomorrow. | Ella vendrá mañana. |
| They will finish the project. | Ellos terminarán el proyecto. |
| We will be there soon. | Estaremos allí pronto. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + will not + Base Verb**

### Contraction

**will not = won't**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I won't forget. | No olvidaré. |
| She will not agree. | Ella no estará de acuerdo. |
| They won't arrive late. | Ellos no llegarán tarde. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Will + Subject + Base Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Will you help me? | ¿Me ayudarás? |
| Will she come with us? | ¿Vendrá con nosotros? |
| Will they finish today? | ¿Terminarán hoy? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I will. | Sí. |
| No, I won't. | No. |
| Yes, she will. | Sí. |
| No, they won't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of Will

## 1. Predictions

Talking about what we think will happen.

Hablar sobre lo que creemos que sucederá.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I think it will rain. | Creo que lloverá. |
| People will live longer. | La gente vivirá más tiempo. |

---

## 2. Spontaneous Decisions

Decisions made at the moment of speaking.

Decisiones tomadas en el momento de hablar.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I will answer the phone. | Contestaré el teléfono. |
| I'll help you with that. | Te ayudaré con eso. |

---

## 3. Promises

Making commitments.

Hacer promesas.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I will always love you. | Siempre te amaré. |
| I won't tell anyone. | No se lo diré a nadie. |

---

## 4. Offers

Offering help or services.

Ofrecer ayuda o servicios.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I will carry your bag. | Llevaré tu bolso. |
| Will I open the window for you? | ¿Te abro la ventana? |

---

## 5. Future Facts

Talking about things that are expected to happen.

Hablar sobre hechos futuros.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| The sun will rise tomorrow. | El sol saldrá mañana. |
| The meeting will begin at ten. | La reunión comenzará a las diez. |

# 🔑 Common Expressions with Will

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I will... | Haré... |

| I won't... | No haré... |

| I think... will... | Creo que... |

---

# ⚖️ Will vs Going To

| Will | Going To |
|-------|------------|
| Prediction or instant decision | Plan or evidence |
| I think it will rain. | Look! It is going to rain. |
| I'll help you. | I am going to study tonight. |

---

# ⚖️ Will vs Shall

| Will | Shall |
|-------|--------|
| Common in modern English | Formal or traditional |
| I will call you. | Shall we dance? |
| Used with all subjects | Mostly with I and we |

---

# 📍 Rules for Modal Verbs

After **will**, always use the base form of the verb.

Después de **will**, siempre se usa el verbo en su forma base.

### Correct

- I will help.
- She will arrive.
- They will study.

### Incorrect

- I will to help.
- She will arrives.
- They will studying.

---

# 📍 Will Have

**Will have + Past Participle** forms the Future Perfect or expresses a future completed action.

**Will have + Participio Pasado** forma el Future Perfect o expresa una acción futura completada.

### Examples

- I will have finished by noon.
- She will have arrived before us.
- They will have completed the project.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I will to help you. | I will help you. |
>| She will goes home. | She will go home. |
>| Will you to come? | Will you come? |
>| They will studying tomorrow. | They will study tomorrow. |
>| He wills arrive soon. | He will arrive soon. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - After **will**, always use the base form.
> - Never use **to** after **will**.
> - Modal verbs never take **-s**, **-ed**, or **-ing**.
> - The main verb never changes after a modal verb.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Sophia: I can't carry all these books.

Daniel: Don't worry. I will help you.

Sophia: Thanks! I think this class will be difficult.

Daniel: Maybe, but we will learn a lot.

Sophia: Will you study with me for the exam?

Daniel: Of course. I won't leave you alone.

Sophia: Great! I promise I will work hard too.

Daniel: Perfect. I'm sure we will pass the exam.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- Predictions
- Spontaneous decisions
- Promises
- Offers
- Future facts

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + will + Base Verb

### Negative

Subject + will not (won't) + Base Verb

### Question

Will + Subject + Base Verb?

## Common Expressions

- I will...
- I'll...
- I won't...
- Will you...?
- I think... will...

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **will** for predictions and spontaneous decisions.
> - Use **will** for promises and offers.
> - After **will**, always use the base form of the verb.
> - Never use **to** after **will**.
> - Use **won't** as the contraction of **will not**.
> - Do not confuse the modal verb **will** with planned actions that are better expressed with **going to**.
