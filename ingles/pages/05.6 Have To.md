

> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** **Have to** is used to express **obligation, necessity, rules, and responsibilities**, usually because of external circumstances.
>
> **Español:** **Have to** se usa para expresar **obligación, necesidad, reglas y responsabilidades**, generalmente debido a circunstancias externas.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I have to work tomorrow.
> - She has to wear a uniform.
> - Do we have to pay now?
> - They don't have to come early.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + have/has to + Base Verb + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have to study tonight. | Tengo que estudiar esta noche. |
| She has to wake up early. | Ella tiene que levantarse temprano. |
| They have to wear uniforms. | Ellos tienen que usar uniforme. |
| We have to finish the project. | Tenemos que terminar el proyecto. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + do/does not + have to + Base Verb**

### Meaning

**Don't have to** means **it is not necessary**.

**Don't have to** significa **no es necesario**.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I don't have to work today. | No tengo que trabajar hoy. |
| She doesn't have to come early. | Ella no tiene que venir temprano. |
| They don't have to wear uniforms on Fridays. | Ellos no tienen que usar uniforme los viernes. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Do/Does + Subject + have to + Base Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Do you have to study tonight? | ¿Tienes que estudiar esta noche? |
| Does she have to work tomorrow? | ¿Ella tiene que trabajar mañana? |
| Do they have to pay now? | ¿Tienen que pagar ahora? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I do. | Sí. |
| No, I don't. | No. |
| Yes, she does. | Sí. |
| No, they don't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of Have To

## 1. External Obligation

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have to wear a uniform at school. | Tengo que usar uniforme en la escuela. |
| She has to follow the company rules. | Ella tiene que seguir las reglas de la empresa. |

---

## 2. Necessity

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| We have to buy food. | Tenemos que comprar comida. |
| He has to see a doctor. | Él tiene que ver a un médico. |

---

## 3. Rules and Requirements

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Visitors have to show identification. | Los visitantes tienen que mostrar identificación. |
| Students have to complete the assignment. | Los estudiantes tienen que completar la tarea. |

---

## 4. Daily Responsibilities

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have to clean my room. | Tengo que limpiar mi habitación. |
| They have to take care of their children. | Ellos tienen que cuidar a sus hijos. |

# ⚖️ Have To vs Must

| Have To | Must |
|----------|-------|
| External obligation | Personal or strong obligation |
| I have to wear a uniform. | I must study harder. |
| Rule or situation | Speaker's decision |

### Examples

- I have to pay my taxes.
- I must improve my English.

---

# ⚖️ Have To vs Should

| Have To | Should |
|----------|----------|
| Obligation | Advice |
| You have to finish your work. | You should finish your work. |
| Necessary | Recommended |

---

# 📍 Have To in Different Tenses

One advantage of **have to** is that it can easily be used in different tenses.

Una ventaja de **have to** es que puede usarse fácilmente en diferentes tiempos verbales.

| Tense | Example |
|--------|----------|
| Present | I have to study. |
| Past | I had to study. |
| Future | I will have to study. |
| Present Perfect | I have had to study a lot. |

---

# 📍 Don't Have To vs Mustn't

This is a very important difference.

Esta es una diferencia muy importante.

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|
| You don't have to come. | It is optional. |
| You mustn't come. | It is prohibited. |

### Examples

- You don't have to wear a tie.
- You mustn't park here.

---

# 📍 Third Person Singular

Remember:

- I have to
- You have to
- We have to
- They have to
- **He has to**
- **She has to**
- **It has to**

### Examples

- She has to leave now.
- He has to work on weekends.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| She have to study. | She has to study. |
>| I don't have study. | I don't have to study. |
>| Do you have to to work? | Do you have to work? |
>| They doesn't have to come. | They don't have to come. |
>| You mustn't come tomorrow. (meaning: optional) | You don't have to come tomorrow. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - Use **has to** with **he, she, it**.
> - Always include **to** after **have**.
> - Use **don't have to** for lack of necessity.
> - Use **mustn't** only for prohibition.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Ava: Do you have to work tomorrow?

Leo: Yes, I do. I have to be at the office at eight.

Ava: That's early. Do you have to wear a suit?

Leo: No, I don't. I only have to wear a company shirt.

Ava: I have to study for my English exam this weekend.

Leo: Really? How many hours do you have to study?

Ava: I think I have to study at least three hours every day.

Leo: Good luck! You don't have to worry. You always do well.

Ava: Thanks! I just have to stay focused.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- External obligation
- Necessity
- Rules and requirements
- Daily responsibilities

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + have/has to + Base Verb

### Negative

Subject + do/does not + have to + Base Verb

### Question

Do/Does + Subject + have to + Base Verb?

## Common Expressions

- I have to...
- You have to...
- Do I have to...?
- I don't have to...

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **have to** for obligations caused by rules or circumstances.
> - Use **has to** with **he, she, it**.
> - Use **don't have to** when something is optional.
> - Do not confuse **don't have to** with **mustn't**.
> - After **have to**, always use the base form of the verb.
> - **Have to** can easily be used in past, present, and future tenses.
