

> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** **May** is a modal verb used to express **permission, possibility, and formal requests**.
>
> **Español:** **May** es un verbo modal que se usa para expresar **permiso, posibilidad y solicitudes formales**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - May I come in?
> - You may leave now.
> - It may rain tomorrow.
> - She may be at home.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + may + Base Verb + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| You may enter. | Puedes entrar. |
| She may come later. | Ella puede venir más tarde. |
| It may snow tomorrow. | Puede que nieve mañana. |
| They may join us. | Puede que ellos se unan a nosotros. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + may not + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| You may not enter this room. | No puedes entrar a esta sala. |
| She may not come today. | Puede que ella no venga hoy. |
| They may not agree. | Puede que ellos no estén de acuerdo. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**May + Subject + Base Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| May I come in? | ¿Puedo entrar? |
| May we ask a question? | ¿Podemos hacer una pregunta? |
| May I use your phone? | ¿Puedo usar tu teléfono? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, you may. | Sí, puedes. |
| No, you may not. | No, no puedes. |

# 📅 Uses of May

## 1. Asking for Permission

More formal than **can**.

Más formal que **can**.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| May I sit here? | ¿Puedo sentarme aquí? |
| May I ask something? | ¿Puedo preguntar algo? |

# ⚖️ May vs Can

| May | Can |
|-----|-----|
| Formal permission | Informal permission |
| May I come in? | Can I come in? |
| More polite | More common |

---

# ⚖️ May vs Might

| May | Might |
|-----|--------|
| More likely possibility | Less certain possibility |
| It may rain. | It might rain. |
| She may come. | She might come. |

---

# 📍 Rules for Modal Verbs

After **may**, always use the base form of the verb.

Después de **may**, siempre se usa el verbo en su forma base.

### Correct

- May I enter?
- She may come.
- They may help.

### Incorrect

- May I to enter?
- She may comes.
- They may helping.

---

# 📍 May Not

**May not** can express:

## Lack of Permission

- You may not park here.

## Negative Possibility

- She may not know the answer.

The meaning depends on the context.

El significado depende del contexto.

---

# 📍 May Have

**May have + Past Participle** expresses possibility about the past.

**May have + Participio Pasado** expresa posibilidad sobre el pasado.

### Examples

- She may have forgotten.
- They may have left already.
- He may have missed the bus.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| May I to come in? | May I come in? |
>| She may goes home. | She may go home. |
>| They may coming later. | They may come later. |
>| He mays arrive soon. | He may arrive soon. |
>| May I can use your phone? | May I use your phone? |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - After **may**, use the base form.
> - Never use **to** after **may**.
> - Modal verbs never take **-s**, **-ed**, or **-ing**.
> - Do not combine two modal verbs together.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Teacher: May I have your attention, please?

Students: Yes, of course.

Teacher: You may start the exam now.

Emma: Excuse me, may I ask a question?

Teacher: Yes, you may.

Emma: I think this answer may be incorrect.

Teacher: It may be, so read the question carefully.

Emma: Thank you. My friend may need help too.

Teacher: That's fine. She may ask me as well.

---

## Español

Profesor: ¿Puedo tener su atención, por favor?

Estudiantes: Sí, por supuesto.

Profesor: Ya pueden comenzar el examen.

Emma: Disculpe, ¿puedo hacer una pregunta?

Profesor: Sí, puedes.

Emma: Creo que esta respuesta puede ser incorrecta.

Profesor: Puede ser, así que lee la pregunta con atención.

Emma: Gracias. Mi amiga también puede necesitar ayuda.

Profesor: Está bien. Ella también puede preguntarme.
