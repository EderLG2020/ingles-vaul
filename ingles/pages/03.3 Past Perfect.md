
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** The Past Perfect is used to describe an action that happened before another action or moment in the past.
>
> **Español:** El Pasado Perfecto se usa para describir una acción que ocurrió antes de otra acción o momento en el pasado.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I had finished my homework before dinner.
> - She had left when I arrived.
> - They had already eaten.
> - We had never seen snow before that trip.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + had + Past Participle + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I had finished my work. | Había terminado mi trabajo. |
| She had studied English. | Ella había estudiado inglés. |
| They had left the office. | Ellos habían salido de la oficina. |
| We had seen that movie. | Habíamos visto esa película. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + had not + Past Participle**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I had not finished my homework. | No había terminado mi tarea. |
| She had not arrived yet. | Ella no había llegado todavía. |
| They had not met before. | Ellos no se habían conocido antes. |

### Contractions

| Full Form | Short Form |
|------------|------------|
| I had not | I hadn't |
| You had not | You hadn't |
| He had not | He hadn't |
| She had not | She hadn't |
| We had not | We hadn't |
| They had not | They hadn't |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Had + Subject + Past Participle?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Had you finished? | ¿Habías terminado? |
| Had she left? | ¿Ella había salido? |
| Had they arrived? | ¿Ellos habían llegado? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I had. | Sí. |
| No, I hadn't. | No. |
| Yes, she had. | Sí. |
| No, they hadn't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of the Past Perfect

## 1. One Action Happened Before Another

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I had eaten before I went to school. | Había comido antes de ir a la escuela. |
| She had finished the report before the meeting started. | Ella había terminado el informe antes de que comenzara la reunión. |

---

## 2. A Completed Action Before a Specific Time in the Past

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| By 10 p.m., we had completed the project. | Para las 10 p.m., habíamos terminado el proyecto. |
| They had moved to Lima by 2022. | Se habían mudado a Lima para 2022. |

---

## 3. Past Experiences

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I had never flown before that trip. | Nunca había viajado en avión antes de ese viaje. |
| She had already read the book. | Ella ya había leído el libro. |

---

## 4. Reported Speech

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---------------|----------------|
| "I have finished." | He said that he had finished. |
| "I have seen it." | She said that she had seen it. |

---

# 🔑 Signal Words / Palabras Clave

Common expressions used with the Past Perfect:

- before
- after
- already
- just
- never
- by the time
- until then
- when

### Examples

- She had already left.
- By the time I arrived, they had finished.
- I had never met him before.

---

# ⚖️ Past Perfect vs Simple Past

| Past Perfect | Simple Past |
|--------------|-------------|
| I had finished before she arrived. | She arrived at 8 p.m. |
| They had left when I called. | I called them yesterday. |
| Earlier past action. | Later past action. |

# 📍 By the Time

**By the time** means that one action was completed before another.

**By the time** significa que una acción se completó antes que otra.

### Examples

- By the time I arrived, the movie had started.
- By the time she called, I had gone to bed.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I had went home. | I had gone home. |
>| She had saw the movie. | She had seen the movie. |
>| Had you finished your work? | ✅ Correct |
>| They had not ate dinner. | They had not eaten dinner. |
>| He had finish the project. | He had finished the project. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - The Past Perfect always uses **had + past participle**.
> - Never use the simple past form after **had**.
> - Regular and irregular past participles must be memorized.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

David: Why were you late for class?

Sophia: Because I had missed the bus.

David: Oh, no! Had you woken up late?

Sophia: Yes, I had. My alarm clock had stopped working.

David: What happened after that?

Sophia: I quickly got ready, but by the time I reached the bus stop, the bus had already left.

David: That's unfortunate. Something similar had happened to me last month.

Sophia: Really?

David: Yes. I had forgotten to charge my phone, so my alarm didn't ring.

Sophia: I guess we both learned an important lesson.

David: Exactly. I had bought a new alarm clock after that day.

---

## Español

David: ¿Por qué llegaste tarde a clase?

Sophia: Porque había perdido el autobús.

David: ¡Oh, no! ¿Te habías despertado tarde?

Sophia: Sí. Mi despertador había dejado de funcionar.

David: ¿Qué pasó después?

Sophia: Me preparé rápidamente, pero cuando llegué a la parada, el autobús ya había salido.

David: Qué mala suerte. Algo parecido me había pasado el mes pasado.

Sophia: ¿En serio?

David: Sí. Había olvidado cargar mi teléfono, así que la alarma no sonó.

Sophia: Supongo que ambos aprendimos una lección importante.

David: Exactamente. Después de ese día compré un nuevo despertador.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- One action before another in the past
- Completed action before a specific past time
- Past experiences
- Reported speech

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + had + Past Participle

### Negative

Subject + had not + Past Participle

### Question

Had + Subject + Past Participle?

## Signal Words

- before
- after
- already
- just
- never
- by the time

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **had + past participle**.
> - The Past Perfect describes the earlier of two past actions.
> - Use **by the time** to show sequence.
> - Never use the simple past after **had**.
> - Learn the most common irregular past participles.
> - The Past Perfect helps make the order of past events clear.
