
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** **Must** is a modal verb used to express **strong obligation, necessity, rules, and logical conclusions**.
>
> **Español:** **Must** es un verbo modal que se usa para expresar **obligación fuerte, necesidad, reglas y conclusiones lógicas**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - You must wear a seatbelt.
> - I must finish my homework.
> - Students must follow the rules.
> - She isn't here. She must be sick.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + must + Base Verb + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I must study tonight. | Debo estudiar esta noche. |
| You must wear a helmet. | Debes usar casco. |
| She must finish the report. | Ella debe terminar el informe. |
| They must arrive on time. | Ellos deben llegar a tiempo. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + must not + Base Verb**

### Contraction

**must not = mustn't**

### Important

**Mustn't** means **it is prohibited**.

**Mustn't** significa **está prohibido**.

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| You mustn't smoke here. | No debes fumar aquí. |
| Students mustn't use phones during the exam. | Los estudiantes no deben usar teléfonos durante el examen. |
| We mustn't touch that machine. | No debemos tocar esa máquina. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

Questions with **must** are possible but are less common than **have to**.

Las preguntas con **must** son posibles, pero son menos comunes que con **have to**.

### Formula / Fórmula

**Must + Subject + Base Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Must I finish this today? | ¿Debo terminar esto hoy? |
| Must we wait here? | ¿Debemos esperar aquí? |

### More Natural Alternatives

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Do I have to finish this today? | ¿Tengo que terminar esto hoy? |
| Do we have to wait here? | ¿Tenemos que esperar aquí? |

# 📅 Uses of Must

## 1. Strong Obligation

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I must finish my project. | Debo terminar mi proyecto. |
| You must study for the exam. | Debes estudiar para el examen. |

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Passengers must wear seatbelts. | Los pasajeros deben usar cinturón de seguridad. |

Usar **must** para expresar una conclusión lógica.

# ⚖️ Must vs Have To

| Must | Have To |
|-------|----------|
| Personal obligation | External obligation |
| I must study. | I have to work. |
| Speaker's decision | Rule or circumstance |

### Examples

- I must eat healthier.
- I have to wear a uniform at work.

---

# ⚖️ Must vs Should

| Must | Should |
|-------|----------|
| Strong obligation | Advice |
| You must stop. | You should stop. |
| Necessary | Recommended |

---

# 📍 Mustn't vs Don't Have To

This is one of the most important differences in English.

Esta es una de las diferencias más importantes en inglés.

| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|-----------|
| You mustn't park here. | It is prohibited. |
| You don't have to park here. | It is not necessary. |

### Examples

- You mustn't cheat on the exam.
- You don't have to come tomorrow.

---

# 📍 Rules for Modal Verbs

After **must**, always use the base form of the verb.

Después de **must**, siempre se usa el verbo en su forma base.

### Correct

- You must study.
- She must leave.
- They must wait.

### Incorrect

- You must to study.
- She must leaves.
- They must waiting.

---

# 📍 Must Have

**Must have + Past Participle** expresses a logical conclusion about the past.

**Must have + Participio Pasado** expresa una conclusión lógica sobre el pasado.

### Examples

- She isn't here. She must have left.
- They look happy. They must have won.
- He must have forgotten the meeting.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| You must to study. | You must study. |
>| She must goes home. | She must go home. |
>| Must I to finish this? | Must I finish this? |
>| They mustn't smoking here. | They mustn't smoke here. |
>| He musts work harder. | He must work harder. |
>| You mustn't come if you don't want. (meaning: not necessary) | You don't have to come if you don't want. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - After **must**, use the base form.
> - Never use **to** after **must**.
> - **Mustn't** means prohibition, not lack of necessity.
> - Use **don't have to** when something is optional.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Ethan: I have an important exam tomorrow.

Mia: Then you must study tonight.

Ethan: I know. I also have to finish my homework.

Mia: And you mustn't stay up too late.

Ethan: You're right. I need some rest too.

Mia: The teacher said we must arrive early.

Ethan: Really? Then we must leave home before seven.

Mia: Yes. And don't worry. You studied a lot, so you must be ready.

Ethan: I hope so. Thanks for the advice.
