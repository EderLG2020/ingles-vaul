

> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** The Present Simple can be used to talk about the future when referring to **schedules, timetables, calendars, and fixed events**.
>
> **Español:** El Presente Simple puede usarse para hablar del futuro cuando nos referimos a **horarios, itinerarios, calendarios y eventos programados**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - The train leaves at 8 a.m.
> - My class starts tomorrow.
> - The movie begins at 7 p.m.
> - The plane arrives at noon.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + Base Verb (or Verb-s/es) + Future Time Expression**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| The bus arrives at 9 a.m. | El autobús llega a las 9 a.m. |
| School starts next Monday. | La escuela comienza el próximo lunes. |
| The meeting finishes at 5 p.m. | La reunión termina a las 5 p.m. |
| My flight leaves tomorrow morning. | Mi vuelo sale mañana por la mañana. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + do/does not + Base Verb**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| The store does not open on Sundays. | La tienda no abre los domingos. |
| The train doesn't stop here. | El tren no se detiene aquí. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Do/Does + Subject + Base Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| When does the movie start? | ¿Cuándo comienza la película? |
| What time does the train leave? | ¿A qué hora sale el tren? |
| Does the class begin at 8 a.m.? | ¿La clase comienza a las 8 a.m.? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, it does. | Sí. |
| No, it doesn't. | No. |

- this evening
- tonight

### Examples

# ⚖️ Present Simple for Future vs Will

| Present Simple | Will |
|----------------|------|
| Fixed schedule | Future decision or prediction |
| The train leaves at 8. | I will take the train. |
| Official timetable | Personal choice |

---

# ⚖️ Present Simple for Future vs Going To

| Present Simple | Going To |
|----------------|-----------|
| Fixed event | Personal plan |
| The movie starts at 7. | I am going to watch the movie. |
| Public schedule | Personal intention |

# 📍 Future Time Clauses

The Present Simple is also used after words like:

- when
- before
- after
- until
- as soon as

### Examples

- I will call you when I arrive.
- We will eat after the movie ends.
- She will wait until you come back.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| The train will leaves at 8. | The train leaves at 8. |
>| My class is start tomorrow. | My class starts tomorrow. |
>| I will call you when I will arrive. | I will call you when I arrive. |
>| The movie begin at 7. | The movie begins at 7. |
>| The flight leave tomorrow. | The flight leaves tomorrow. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - Use the Present Simple for schedules.
> - After **when**, **before**, **after**, and **until**, use the Present Simple for future meaning.
> - Remember to add **-s** or **-es** for third person singular.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Sophia: What time does your flight leave tomorrow?

James: It leaves at 9 a.m.

Sophia: And when does it arrive?

James: It arrives at noon.

Sophia: What are you going to do after you arrive?

James: I will visit my family after the plane lands.

Sophia: That sounds nice. When does your vacation end?

James: It ends next Sunday, and my return flight leaves early in the morning.

Sophia: I hope you have a great trip.

James: Thank you!

---

## Español

Sophia: ¿A qué hora sale tu vuelo mañana?

James: Sale a las 9 a.m.

Sophia: ¿Y cuándo llega?

James: Llega al mediodía.

Sophia: ¿Qué vas a hacer después de llegar?

James: Visitaré a mi familia después de que el avión aterrice.

Sophia: Suena bien. ¿Cuándo termina tu viaje?

James: Termina el próximo domingo y mi vuelo de regreso sale temprano por la mañana.

Sophia: Espero que tengas un gran viaje.

James: ¡Gracias!

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- Timetables
- Public schedules
- Fixed events
- Future time clauses

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + Base Verb (+ s/es)

### Negative

Subject + do/does not + Base Verb

### Question

Do/Does + Subject + Base Verb?

## Signal Words

- tomorrow
- next week
- at 8 a.m.
- on Monday
- when
- before
- after

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use the Present Simple for official schedules and timetables.
> - Use it after **when**, **before**, **after**, **until**, and **as soon as** when talking about the future.
> - Do not use **will** inside future time clauses.
> - Remember the **-s / -es** ending for third person singular.
> - Use **will** for decisions and predictions, not for fixed schedules.
> - The Present Simple for Future is common in transportation, calendars, and event planning.
