
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** The Present Perfect Continuous is used for actions that started in the past and are still happening or have recently stopped, with emphasis on the duration of the action.
>
> **Español:** El Presente Perfecto Continuo se usa para acciones que comenzaron en el pasado y continúan hasta ahora o acaban de terminar, enfatizando la duración de la acción.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I have been studying for two hours.
> - She has been working all day.
> - They have been waiting since morning.
> - We have been learning English for six months.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + have/has + been + Verb-ing**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have been studying. | He estado estudiando. |
| She has been working. | Ella ha estado trabajando. |
| We have been waiting. | Hemos estado esperando. |
| They have been traveling. | Han estado viajando. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + have/has not + been + Verb-ing**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have not been sleeping well. | No he estado durmiendo bien. |
| She has not been feeling good. | Ella no se ha estado sintiendo bien. |
| They have not been studying. | Ellos no han estado estudiando. |

### Contractions

| Full Form | Short Form |
|------------|------------|
| I have not been | I haven't been |
| He has not been | He hasn't been |
| We have not been | We haven't been |
| They have not been | They haven't been |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Have/Has + Subject + been + Verb-ing?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Have you been studying? | ¿Has estado estudiando? |
| Has she been working? | ¿Ha estado trabajando? |
| Have they been waiting long? | ¿Han estado esperando mucho tiempo? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I have. | Sí. |
| No, I haven't. | No. |
| Yes, she has. | Sí. |
| No, she hasn't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of the Present Perfect Continuous

## 1. Actions That Started in the Past and Continue Now

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have been living here for five years. | He estado viviendo aquí durante cinco años. |
| She has been working at this company since 2021. | Ella ha estado trabajando en esta empresa desde 2021. |

---

## 2. Long Activities Still in Progress

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| They have been studying all afternoon. | Han estado estudiando toda la tarde. |
| We have been practicing English every day. | Hemos estado practicando inglés todos los días. |

---

## 3. Recent Activities With Visible Results

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| You look tired. Have you been running? | Te ves cansado. ¿Has estado corriendo? |
| The ground is wet. It has been raining. | El suelo está mojado. Ha estado lloviendo. |

# 🔄 For and Since

## For = Duration

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| for two hours | durante dos horas |
| for a week | durante una semana |
| for ten years | durante diez años |

### Examples

- I have been studying for three hours.
- She has been waiting for a long time.

---

## Since = Starting Point

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| since Monday | desde el lunes |
| since 2020 | desde 2020 |
| since this morning | desde esta mañana |

### Examples

- They have been working since 8 a.m.
- I have been learning English since last year.

# 🔑 Signal Words / Palabras Clave

Common expressions used with the Present Perfect Continuous:

- all morning
- lately
- recently

- for a long time

### Examples

- I have been working all day.
- She has been studying lately.
- How long have you been waiting?

# ⚖️ Present Perfect vs Present Perfect Continuous

| Present Perfect | Present Perfect Continuous |
|----------------|----------------------------|
| Focus on the result. | Focus on the duration. |
| I have written five emails. | I have been writing emails all morning. |
| She has cleaned the house. | She has been cleaning the house for two hours. |

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I have been study. | I have been studying. |
>| She has been work all day. | She has been working all day. |
>| Have you been wait long? | Have you been waiting long? |
>| He have been studying. | He has been studying. |
>| They has been playing. | They have been playing. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - The structure is always **have/has + been + verb-ing**.
> - The main verb must end in **-ing**.
> - Use **has** only with **he, she, it**.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

Emma: Really? How long have you been studying?

Daniel: I have been studying since eight o'clock this morning.

Emma: Wow! Have you been taking breaks?

Emma: That's great. I have been learning English too, but I have been focusing on vocabulary.

Daniel: Good idea. We have been improving a lot this year.

Emma: Yes, we have. Let's keep practicing!

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- Actions that started in the past and continue now
- Long activities still in progress
- Recent actions with visible results

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + have/has + been + Verb-ing

### Negative

Subject + have/has not + been + Verb-ing

### Question

Have/Has + Subject + been + Verb-ing?

## Signal Words

- for
- since
- all day
- lately
- recently
- how long

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **have/has + been + verb-ing**.
> - Focus on the duration of the action.
> - Use **for** with periods of time.
> - Use **since** with starting points.
> - The action usually started in the past and continues until now.
> - Do not confuse it with the Present Perfect, which focuses more on the result than the process.
