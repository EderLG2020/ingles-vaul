

> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** Time expressions tell us **when**, **how often**, or **how long** an action happens.
>
> **Español:** Las expresiones de tiempo nos dicen **cuándo**, **con qué frecuencia** o **durante cuánto tiempo** ocurre una acción.

> - She is working now.

# 🕒 What Are Time Expressions? / ¿Qué Son las Expresiones de Tiempo?

Time expressions help us understand when an action happens.

Las expresiones de tiempo nos ayudan a entender cuándo ocurre una acción.

They can express:

- Present
- Past
- Future
- Frequency
- Duration

---

# 📍 Position in the Sentence

Time expressions usually go at the beginning or end of the sentence.

Las expresiones de tiempo normalmente van al principio o al final de la oración.

### End Position

- I study English every day.
- She arrived yesterday.
- We will travel next month.

### Beginning Position

- Yesterday, I visited my friend.
- Tomorrow, we will have a meeting.
- Today, I am very busy.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I am studying every day. | I study every day. |
>| I have seen him yesterday. | I saw him yesterday. |
>| She works now. | She is working now. |
>| I live here since 2020. | I have lived here since 2020. |
>| They arrived ago two hours. | They arrived two hours ago. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - Some time expressions are linked to specific tenses.
> - **Yesterday** is used with the Simple Past.
> - **Now** is commonly used with the Present Continuous.
> - **Since** and **for** are often used with the Present Perfect.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Oliver: Hi, Mia! What are you doing now?

Mia: I am studying for my English test.

Oliver: Do you study every day?

Mia: Yes, I usually study for one hour after school.

Oliver: Have you been learning English for a long time?

Mia: Yes, I have been learning it since 2023.

Oliver: Great! Are you doing anything tomorrow?

Mia: Yes, I am meeting my classmates to practice speaking.

Oliver: That sounds like a good idea. I often practice with my friends too.

---

## Español

Oliver: ¡Hola, Mia! ¿Qué estás haciendo ahora?

Mia: Estoy estudiando para mi examen de inglés.

Oliver: ¿Estudias todos los días?

Mia: Sí, normalmente estudio una hora después de clases.

Oliver: ¿Has estado aprendiendo inglés durante mucho tiempo?

Mia: Sí, lo he estado aprendiendo desde 2023.

Oliver: ¡Excelente! ¿Harás algo mañana?

Mia: Sí, me reuniré con mis compañeros para practicar conversación.

Oliver: Suena como una buena idea. Yo también practico a menudo con mis amigos.
