
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** The Past Perfect Continuous is used for actions that had been happening before another action or moment in the past, emphasizing the duration of the activity.
>
> **Español:** El Pasado Perfecto Continuo se usa para acciones que habían estado ocurriendo antes de otra acción o momento en el pasado, enfatizando la duración de la actividad.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I had been studying for three hours before the exam.
> - She had been working all day.
> - They had been waiting since morning.
> - We had been living there for ten years.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + had + been + Verb-ing + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I had been studying. | Había estado estudiando. |
| She had been working. | Ella había estado trabajando. |
| They had been waiting. | Ellos habían estado esperando. |
| We had been traveling. | Habíamos estado viajando. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + had not + been + Verb-ing**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I had not been sleeping well. | No había estado durmiendo bien. |
| She had not been feeling well. | Ella no se había estado sintiendo bien. |
| They had not been practicing. | Ellos no habían estado practicando. |

### Contractions

| Full Form | Short Form |
|------------|------------|
| I had not been | I hadn't been |
| She had not been | She hadn't been |
| We had not been | We hadn't been |
| They had not been | They hadn't been |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Had + Subject + been + Verb-ing?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Had you been studying? | ¿Habías estado estudiando? |
| Had she been working? | ¿Había estado trabajando? |
| Had they been waiting long? | ¿Habían estado esperando mucho tiempo? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I had. | Sí. |
| No, I hadn't. | No. |
| Yes, she had. | Sí. |
| No, they hadn't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of the Past Perfect Continuous

## 1. Long Actions Before Another Past Action

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I had been studying for hours before the exam started. | Había estado estudiando durante horas antes de que comenzara el examen. |
| She had been working all day before she went home. | Ella había estado trabajando todo el día antes de ir a casa. |

---

## 2. Emphasizing Duration

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| They had been waiting for two hours. | Habían estado esperando durante dos horas. |
| We had been driving since sunrise. | Habíamos estado conduciendo desde el amanecer. |

---

## 3. Showing the Cause of a Past Situation

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| He was tired because he had been running. | Estaba cansado porque había estado corriendo. |
| The streets were wet because it had been raining. | Las calles estaban mojadas porque había estado lloviendo. |

# ⚖️ Past Perfect vs Past Perfect Continuous

| Past Perfect | Past Perfect Continuous |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Focus on the result. | Focus on the duration. |
| I had finished my homework. | I had been doing my homework for two hours. |
| She had cleaned the house. | She had been cleaning the house all morning. |

---

# 📍 Past Perfect Continuous vs Past Continuous

| Past Perfect Continuous | Past Continuous |
|-------------------------|-----------------|
| Action started before another past event. | Action was happening at a past moment. |
| I had been waiting for an hour when the bus arrived. | I was waiting for the bus at 8 p.m. |

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I had been study. | I had been studying. |
>| She had been work all day. | She had been working all day. |
>| They had been wait for hours. | They had been waiting for hours. |
>| Had you been sleeping? | ✅ Correct |
>| He had been play soccer. | He had been playing soccer. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - The structure is always **had + been + verb-ing**.
> - The main verb must end in **-ing**.
> - This tense emphasizes the duration of an activity before another past event.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Daniel: You looked very tired yesterday. What happened?

Laura: I had been studying for my final exams all week.

Daniel: Really? How many hours had you been studying every day?

Laura: Around six hours. I had also been attending extra classes.

Daniel: No wonder you were exhausted.

Laura: Yes, and by the time the exams started, I had been sleeping only a few hours each night.

Daniel: I understand. Last semester, I had been working during the day and studying at night.

Laura: That must have been difficult.

Daniel: It was, but all the effort was worth it.

Laura: I agree. We had been working hard, and finally everything went well.

## Español

Daniel: Ayer te veías muy cansada. ¿Qué pasó?

Laura: Había estado estudiando para mis exámenes finales toda la semana.

Daniel: ¿En serio? ¿Cuántas horas habías estado estudiando cada día?

Laura: Alrededor de seis horas. También había estado asistiendo a clases adicionales.

Daniel: Con razón estabas agotada.

Laura: Sí, y para cuando comenzaron los exámenes, había estado durmiendo solo unas pocas horas cada noche.

Daniel: Te entiendo. El semestre pasado, yo había estado trabajando durante el día y estudiando por las noches.

Laura: Debió ser difícil.

Daniel: Lo fue, pero todo el esfuerzo valió la pena.

Laura: Estoy de acuerdo. Habíamos estado trabajando duro y finalmente todo salió bien.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- Long actions before another past action
- Emphasizing duration
- Explaining the cause of a past situation

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + had + been + Verb-ing

### Negative

Subject + had not + been + Verb-ing

### Question

Had + Subject + been + Verb-ing?

## Signal Words

- for
- since
- before
- all day
- all night
- by the time

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **had + been + verb-ing**.
> - Focus on the duration of the activity.
> - Use **for** for periods of time.
> - Use **since** for starting points.
> - The action happened before another event in the past.
> - Do not confuse it with the Past Perfect, which focuses on the result rather than the process.
