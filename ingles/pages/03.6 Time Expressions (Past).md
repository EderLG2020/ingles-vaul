
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** Past time expressions tell us **when a past action happened**. They are commonly used with the **Simple Past**, **Past Continuous**, **Past Perfect**, and **Past Perfect Continuous**.
>
> **Español:** Las expresiones de tiempo en pasado nos indican **cuándo ocurrió una acción pasada**. Se usan comúnmente con el **Pasado Simple**, **Pasado Continuo**, **Pasado Perfecto** y **Pasado Perfecto Continuo**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I visited my grandparents yesterday.
> - She was studying last night.
> - They had already left.
> - We had been waiting for two hours.

# 🕒 What Are Past Time Expressions? / ¿Qué Son las Expresiones de Tiempo en Pasado?

Past time expressions indicate that an action happened before the present moment.

Las expresiones de tiempo en pasado indican que una acción ocurrió antes del momento presente.

They help answer questions such as:

- When?
- How long ago?
- For how long?
- Before what event?

# ⚖️ Ago vs Before

## Ago

Used to count back from the present.

Se usa para contar hacia atrás desde el presente.

### Examples

- I arrived two days ago.
- She graduated five years ago.

---

## Before

Used to show that one action happened earlier than another.

Se usa para indicar que una acción ocurrió antes que otra.

### Examples

- I had finished before dinner.
- She left before I arrived.

---

# ⚖️ Last vs Ago

| Last | Ago |
|-------|-----|
| last week | a week ago |
| last month | a month ago |
| last year | a year ago |

### Examples

- I traveled last week.
- I traveled a week ago.

Both are correct, but the structure is different.

---

# 📍 By the Time

**By the time** connects two past actions.

**By the time** conecta dos acciones pasadas.

### Examples

- By the time I arrived, the movie had started.
- By the time she called, we had gone home.

---

# 📍 While vs When

## While

Used with long actions.

Se usa con acciones largas.

- I was reading while my brother was studying.

---

## When

Often introduces a short action.

A menudo introduce una acción corta.

- I was sleeping when the phone rang.

---

# 📍 In the Past vs Ago

| Expression | Use |
|------------|------|
| ago | exact distance from now |
| in the past | general past time |

### Examples

- I met him ten years ago.
- People traveled differently in the past.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I have seen him yesterday. | I saw him yesterday. |
>| I arrived before two days. | I arrived two days ago. |
>| She had left ago. | She had left before. |
>| I was study when you called. | I was studying when you called. |
>| They had been work all day. | They had been working all day. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - **Yesterday** is used with the Simple Past.
> - **Ago** measures time from the present.
> - **Before** compares two past actions.
> - **While** and **when** are commonly used with the Past Continuous.
> - Some expressions naturally fit specific tenses.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Nathan: What did you do last weekend?

Sophie: I visited my grandparents.

Nathan: Really? When did you arrive?

Sophie: I arrived on Friday evening.

Nathan: What were you doing when I sent you a message?

Sophie: I was having dinner with my family.

Nathan: Had you seen your grandparents recently?

Sophie: No, I hadn't seen them for six months.

Nathan: What had they been doing?

Sophie: They had been renovating their house for several weeks.

Nathan: That sounds like a busy weekend.

Sophie: It was, but we had a wonderful time together.

Sophie: No, no los había visto durante seis meses.

Sophie: Habían estado renovando su casa durante varias semanas.

Nathan: Parece que fue un fin de semana ocupado.

Sophie: Lo fue, pero pasamos un tiempo maravilloso juntos.

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **yesterday** and **ago** with the Simple Past.
> - Use **while** and **when** with the Past Continuous.
> - Use **before** and **by the time** with the Past Perfect.
> - Use **for** and **since** to express duration.
> - Time expressions help clarify the order and duration of past events.
> - Learning these expressions will make your storytelling more natural.
