
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** The Future Perfect is used for actions that **will be completed before a specific time or event in the future**.
>
> **Español:** El Futuro Perfecto se usa para acciones que **se habrán completado antes de un momento o evento específico en el futuro**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I will have finished my homework by 8 p.m.
> - She will have arrived before noon.
> - They will have completed the project by Friday.
> - We will have lived here for ten years by next month.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + will + have + Past Participle + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I will have finished my work. | Habré terminado mi trabajo. |
| She will have arrived by noon. | Ella habrá llegado al mediodía. |
| They will have left before sunset. | Ellos habrán salido antes del atardecer. |
| We will have completed the project. | Habremos completado el proyecto. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + will not + have + Past Participle**

### Contraction

**will not = won't**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I won't have finished by then. | No habré terminado para entonces. |
| She won't have arrived yet. | Ella aún no habrá llegado. |
| They will not have completed the work. | Ellos no habrán terminado el trabajo. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Will + Subject + have + Past Participle?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Will you have finished by tomorrow? | ¿Habrás terminado para mañana? |
| Will she have arrived by 10 a.m.? | ¿Habrá llegado para las 10 a.m.? |
| Will they have completed the project? | ¿Habrán completado el proyecto? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I will. | Sí. |
| No, I won't. | No. |
| Yes, she will. | Sí. |
| No, they won't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of the Future Perfect

## 1. Completed Action Before a Future Time

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I will have finished my homework by 9 p.m. | Habré terminado mi tarea para las 9 p.m. |
| She will have graduated by next year. | Ella se habrá graduado para el próximo año. |

---

## 2. Completed Action Before Another Future Event

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| They will have left before you arrive. | Ellos habrán salido antes de que llegues. |
| We will have eaten when the movie starts. | Habremos comido cuando empiece la película. |

## 3. Achievements and Milestones

| English | Español |

# ⚖️ Future Perfect vs Future Continuous

| Future Perfect | Future Continuous |
|----------------|------------------|
| Focus on completion | Focus on progress |
| I will have finished the book. | I will be reading the book. |
| Action completed before a future time | Action happening at a future time |

---

# ⚖️ Future Perfect vs Present Perfect

| Future Perfect | Present Perfect |
|----------------|----------------|
| I will have finished. | I have finished. |
| Future completion | Present result |

---

# 📍 By and By the Time

## By

Means **before or not later than** a future moment.

Significa **antes de o como máximo en** un momento futuro.

### Examples

- I will have finished by Friday.
- She will have arrived by noon.

---

## By the Time

Connects two future actions.

Conecta dos acciones futuras.

### Examples

- By the time you arrive, I will have cooked dinner.
- By the time the class starts, they will have completed the exercise.

---

# 📖 Past Participles

The Future Perfect always uses a **past participle**.

El Futuro Perfecto siempre usa un **participio pasado**.

| Base Verb | Past Participle |
|------------|----------------|
| work | worked |
| go | gone |
| see | seen |
| eat | eaten |
| write | written |
| do | done |
| take | taken |

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I will have finish my work. | I will have finished my work. |
>| She will has arrived. | She will have arrived. |
>| Will you have finished? | ✅ Correct |
>| They won't have went home. | They won't have gone home. |
>| We will have complete the project. | We will have completed the project. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - The structure is always **will + have + past participle**.
> - Never use the base form after **have**.
> - Learn the most common irregular past participles.

# 📌 Summary / Resumen

## Main Uses

- Completed actions before a future time
- Completed actions before another future event
- Future achievements and milestones

## Basic Structures

### Affirmative

Subject + will + have + Past Participle

### Negative

Subject + will not + have + Past Participle

### Question

Will + Subject + have + Past Participle?

## Signal Words

- by
- by the time
- before
- by tomorrow
- by next year

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **will + have + past participle**.
> - Focus on actions that will be completed.
> - Use **by** and **by the time** to show deadlines.
> - Always use the correct past participle.
> - Do not use the base verb after **have**.
> - The Future Perfect emphasizes the result before a future moment.
