
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** The Present Perfect connects the past with the present. It describes experiences, recent actions, and situations that continue until now.
>
> **Español:** El Presente Perfecto conecta el pasado con el presente. Describe experiencias, acciones recientes y situaciones que continúan hasta ahora.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I have finished my homework.
> - She has visited London.
> - They have lived here for five years.
> - We have just arrived.

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + have/has + Past Participle + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have finished my work. | He terminado mi trabajo. |
| You have seen that movie. | Has visto esa película. |
| He has lost his keys. | Él ha perdido sus llaves. |
| She has studied English. | Ella ha estudiado inglés. |
| We have arrived. | Hemos llegado. |
| They have traveled a lot. | Ellos han viajado mucho. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + have/has not + Past Participle**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have not finished. | No he terminado. |
| She has not called me. | Ella no me ha llamado. |
| They have not arrived yet. | Ellos no han llegado todavía. |

### Contractions

| Full Form | Short Form |
|------------|------------|
| I have not | I haven't |
| You have not | You haven't |
| He has not | He hasn't |
| She has not | She hasn't |
| We have not | We haven't |
| They have not | They haven't |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Have/Has + Subject + Past Participle?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Have you finished? | ¿Has terminado? |
| Has she called you? | ¿Ella te ha llamado? |
| Have they arrived? | ¿Han llegado? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I have. | Sí. |
| No, I haven't. | No. |
| Yes, she has. | Sí. |
| No, she hasn't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of the Present Perfect

## 1. Life Experiences / Experiencias de Vida

We do not say exactly when the action happened.

No decimos exactamente cuándo ocurrió la acción.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have visited Spain. | He visitado España. |
| She has flown on a plane. | Ella ha viajado en avión. |

---

## 2. Recent Actions / Acciones Recientes

Often used with **just**, **already**, and **yet**.

A menudo se usa con **just**, **already** y **yet**.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have just finished. | Acabo de terminar. |
| She has already left. | Ella ya se fue. |
| Have you eaten yet? | ¿Ya has comido? |

---

## 3. Actions That Continue Until Now

Usually with **for** and **since**.

Normalmente con **for** y **since**.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have lived here for ten years. | He vivido aquí durante diez años. |
| She has worked here since 2020. | Ella ha trabajado aquí desde 2020. |

---

## 4. Unfinished Time Periods

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I have read three books this week. | He leído tres libros esta semana. |
| They have visited us today. | Nos han visitado hoy. |

---

# 🔑 Signal Words / Palabras Clave

Common expressions used with the Present Perfect:

- ever
- never
- just
- already
- yet
- for
- since
- recently
- lately
- so far

### Examples

- Have you ever been to Peru?
- I have never seen that movie.
- She has already finished.
- They haven't arrived yet.

# 🔄 For vs Since

| Word | Use |
|-------|------|
| for | a period of time |
| since | a starting point |

### Examples

- I have lived here for five years.
- I have lived here since 2021.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I have went to school. | I have gone to school. |
>| She have finished. | She has finished. |
>| Have you saw this movie? | Have you seen this movie? |
>| I am here since 2020. | I have been here since 2020. |
>| They has arrived. | They have arrived. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - The Present Perfect uses **have/has + past participle**.
> - Never use the simple past form after **have** or **has**.
> - Use **has** only with **he, she, it**.

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **have/has + past participle**.
> - Use **has** with **he, she, it**.
> - Use **for** for periods of time.
> - Use **since** for starting points.
> - Never use the simple past after **have** or **has**.
> - The Present Perfect connects the past with the present.
