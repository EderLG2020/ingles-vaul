
> [!tip] ⚡ Quick Rule / Regla Rápida
>
> **English:** **Can** is a modal verb used to express **ability, possibility, permission, and requests**.
>
> **Español:** **Can** es un verbo modal que se usa para expresar **habilidad, posibilidad, permiso y solicitudes**.
>
> **Examples:**
>
> - I can swim.
> - She can speak English.
> - Can I open the window?
> - Can you help me?

# 🏗️ Basic Structure / Estructura Básica

## Affirmative / Afirmativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + can + Base Verb + Object**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I can swim. | Puedo nadar. |
| She can drive. | Ella puede conducir. |
| They can play the guitar. | Ellos pueden tocar la guitarra. |
| We can solve the problem. | Podemos resolver el problema. |

---

## Negative / Negativo

### Formula / Fórmula

**Subject + cannot + Base Verb**

### Contraction

**cannot = can't**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I can't swim. | No puedo nadar. |
| She cannot come today. | Ella no puede venir hoy. |
| They can't play soccer. | Ellos no pueden jugar fútbol. |

---

## Questions / Preguntas

### Formula / Fórmula

**Can + Subject + Base Verb?**

### Examples

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Can you swim? | ¿Puedes nadar? |
| Can she drive? | ¿Puede conducir? |
| Can they help us? | ¿Pueden ayudarnos? |

### Short Answers

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Yes, I can. | Sí. |
| No, I can't. | No. |
| Yes, she can. | Sí. |
| No, they can't. | No. |

# 📅 Uses of Can

## 1. Ability

Talking about skills or talents.

Hablar sobre habilidades o talentos.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| I can cook. | Puedo cocinar. |
| She can dance very well. | Ella puede bailar muy bien. |
| They can speak three languages. | Ellos pueden hablar tres idiomas. |

---

## 2. Permission

Asking for or giving permission.

Pedir o dar permiso.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Can I leave early? | ¿Puedo salir temprano? |
| You can use my computer. | Puedes usar mi computadora. |
| Can we sit here? | ¿Podemos sentarnos aquí? |

---

## 3. Requests

Making informal requests.

Hacer solicitudes informales.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Can you help me? | ¿Puedes ayudarme? |
| Can you open the door? | ¿Puedes abrir la puerta? |
| Can you repeat that? | ¿Puedes repetir eso? |

---

## 4. Possibility

Talking about something that is possible.

Hablar sobre algo que es posible.

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| It can be dangerous. | Puede ser peligroso. |
| Learning English can be fun. | Aprender inglés puede ser divertido. |
| This road can get busy. | Esta carretera puede congestionarse. |

---

# 🔑 Common Expressions with Can

| English | Español |
|----------|----------|
| Can I...? | ¿Puedo...? |
| Can you...? | ¿Puedes...? |
| I can. | Puedo. |
| I can't. | No puedo. |
| We can do it. | Podemos hacerlo. |
| Anyone can learn English. | Cualquiera puede aprender inglés. |

---

# ⚖️ Can vs Could

| Can | Could |
|------|--------|
| Present ability | Past ability |
| Informal request | More polite request |
| I can swim. | I could swim when I was five. |
| Can you help me? | Could you help me? |

---

# ⚖️ Can vs Be Able To

| Can | Be Able To |
|------|-------------|
| Common and simple | More formal or flexible |
| I can speak English. | I am able to speak English. |
| She can run fast. | She is able to run fast. |

---

# 📍 Rules for Modal Verbs

After **can**, always use the base form of the verb.

Después de **can**, siempre se usa el verbo en su forma base.

### Correct

- I can swim.
- She can drive.
- They can study.

### Incorrect

- I can to swim.
- She can drives.
- They can studying.

---

# 📍 Cannot vs Can't

| Form | Example |
|-------|----------|
| cannot | I cannot help you today. |
| can't | I can't help you today. |

Both forms are correct.

Ambas formas son correctas.

---

> [!warning] ⚠️ Common Mistakes / Errores Comunes
>
>| Incorrect ❌ | Correct ✅ |
>|--------------|-------------|
>| I can to swim. | I can swim. |
>| She can drives. | She can drive. |
>| Can you to help me? | Can you help me? |
>| They can't playing soccer. | They can't play soccer. |
>| He cans speak English. | He can speak English. |
>
> ### Why? / ¿Por qué?
>
> - After **can**, always use the base form.
> - Never use **to** after **can**.
> - The main verb never changes after a modal verb.
> - Do not add **-s**, **-ed**, or **-ing**.

# 🎭 Conversation Practice / Conversación

## English

Alex: Can you play the guitar?

Emily: Yes, I can. I started learning three years ago.

Alex: That's great! Can you also sing?

Emily: A little, but I can't sing very well.

Alex: Can you help me learn the guitar?

Emily: Of course! We can practice every weekend.

Alex: Wonderful. Can I bring a friend too?

Emily: Yes, you can. The more people, the more fun.

Alex: Thanks! I think we can become a great band one day.

---

## Español

Alex: ¿Puedes tocar la guitarra?

Emily: Sí. Empecé a aprender hace tres años.

Alex: ¡Qué bien! ¿También puedes cantar?

Emily: Un poco, pero no puedo cantar muy bien.

Alex: ¿Puedes ayudarme a aprender guitarra?

Emily: ¡Por supuesto! Podemos practicar todos los fines de semana.

Alex: Genial. ¿Puedo llevar a un amigo también?

Emily: Sí, puedes. Cuantas más personas, más divertido.

Alex: ¡Gracias! Creo que algún día podremos formar una gran banda.

### Negative

Subject + cannot (can't) + Base Verb

> [!success] ⭐ Golden Rules
>
> - Use **can** for present ability.
> - Use **can** to ask for or give permission.
> - Use **can** for informal requests.
> - After **can**, always use the base form of the verb.
> - Never use **to** after **can**.
> - Modal verbs do not change according to the subject.
