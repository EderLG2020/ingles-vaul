# 1. Verb To Be

---

## Definition

The verb **To Be** is the most important verb in English.
It is used to talk about:
- **Identity** → who you are
- **Characteristics** → what something is like
- **Nationality** → where you are from
- **Profession** → what you do
- **Location** → where something is
- **Age** → how old someone is

> In Spanish: equivale a los verbos **ser** y **estar**.

---

## Structure

### Present Tense — Full Forms

| Subject | Verb To Be | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| I | **am** | I am a student. |
| You | **are** | You are my friend. |
| He | **is** | He is a doctor. |
| She | **is** | She is happy. |
| It | **is** | It is a cat. |
| We | **are** | We are from Peru. |
| You (plural) | **are** | You are teachers. |
| They | **are** | They are my parents. |

---

### Contractions (Short Forms)

| Full Form | Contraction |
|-----------|-------------|
| I am | **I'm** |
| You are | **You're** |
| He is | **He's** |
| She is | **She's** |
| It is | **It's** |
| We are | **We're** |
| They are | **They're** |

> **Note:** Contractions are used in spoken English and informal writing. Always use them in conversation.

---

### Negative Form

| Subject | Negative Full | Contraction |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| I | am not | **I'm not** |
| You | are not | **You aren't** |
| He | is not | **He isn't** |
| She | is not | **She isn't** |
| It | is not | **It isn't** |
| We | are not | **We aren't** |
| They | are not | **They aren't** |

---

### Question Form

To make a question, **invert** the subject and the verb:
```

Statement: She is a nurse. Question: Is she a nurse?

Statement: They are from Brazil. Question: Are they from Brazil?

```

| Question | Short Answer (Yes) | Short Answer (No) |
|----------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Am I late? | Yes, you are. | No, you aren't. |
| Are you a student? | Yes, I am. | No, I'm not. |
| Is he tired? | Yes, he is. | No, he isn't. |
| Is she at home? | Yes, she is. | No, she isn't. |
| Are they ready? | Yes, they are. | No, they aren't. |

---

## Rules

1. **"Am"** is only used with **I**.
2. **"Is"** is used with **He, She, It** (singular, third person).
3. **"Are"** is used with **You, We, They** (plural and second person).
4. **Never** use a contraction in a short positive answer.
   - ✅ Yes, I **am**. ❌ Yes, I**'m**.
5. The verb To Be **does not need an auxiliary** to form negatives or questions — it acts as its own auxiliary.

---

## Positive Examples
```

I'm 25 years old. You're very kind. He's a famous actor. She's from Colombia. It's a beautiful day. We're in the library. They're my best friends. The weather is perfect today. My dog is very friendly. Lima is the capital of Peru.

```

---

## Negative Examples
```

I'm not a doctor. You aren't late for class. He isn't married. She isn't at school right now. It isn't cold today. We aren't tired. They aren't from Spain. The movie isn't interesting. My phone isn't new. The answers aren't correct.

```

---

## Questions
```

Am I in the right classroom? Are you from Lima? Is he your brother? Is she a good teacher? Is it expensive? Are we on time? Are they your neighbors? Is the restaurant open? Are your parents at home? Is English difficult?

```

---

## Common Mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| She am happy. | She **is** happy. | "am" is only for "I" |
| He are my friend. | He **is** my friend. | "are" is not for "he/she/it" |
| They is students. | They **are** students. | "is" is not for "they" |
| I am not have a car. | I **don't have** a car. | "To Be" is not used for possession |
| Yes, I'm. | Yes, I **am**. | No contraction in short answers |
| Is she have a dog? | **Does** she have a dog? | Use "does" for other verbs |
| It's = Its | **It's** = It is / **Its** = possessive | Different words, different meanings |

---

## Pronunciation

| Form | Pronunciation | Sound |
|------|--------------|-------|
| am | /æm/ | "am" (like the word) |
| is | /ɪz/ | "iz" |
| are | /ɑːr/ | "ar" |
| I'm | /aɪm/ | "aim" |
| He's / She's / It's | /hiːz/ /ʃiːz/ /ɪts/ | smooth and fast |
| We're | /wɪər/ | "weer" |
| They're | /ðeər/ | "thair" |

> **Tip:** In natural speech, "are" is almost always reduced.
> "You are" sounds like **"you're"** even when not written as a contraction.

> 💡 **Tip 1:** Learn the contractions first. Native speakers almost never say "I am" — they say "I'm". Sound natural from day one.

> 💡 **Tip 4:** To Be is NOT used for actions. "I am have a car" is wrong. For actions, you need other verbs.

> 💡 **Tip 5:** When in doubt between "is" and "are", ask yourself: is the subject singular (is) or plural (are)?

# 2. Subject Pronouns

---

## Definition

Subject pronouns **replace the name of a person or thing** when it is the subject of the sentence — meaning, the one doing the action or being described.

> Instead of saying "**Maria** is my friend", you say "**She** is my friend."

---

## Structure

| Pronoun | Refers to | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **I** | The person speaking (myself) | I am from Peru. |
| **You** | The person being spoken to | You are so funny. |
| **He** | A male person or animal | He is my brother. |
| **She** | A female person or animal | She is a great cook. |
| **It** | A thing, animal (neutral), or idea | It is raining outside. |
| **We** | The speaker + other people | We are a great team. |
| **You** | A group of people | You are all welcome here. |
| **They** | A group of people or things | They are on vacation. |

---

## Rules

1. **"I"** is always written with a capital letter, no matter where it appears in a sentence.
   - ✅ My mom and **I** are going shopping.
2. **"You"** is the same for singular and plural in English.
   - Talking to one person: **You** are my friend.
   - Talking to a group: **You** are all invited.
3. **"It"** is used for things, places, weather, time, and animals (when gender is unknown).
   - **It** is 7 o'clock. / **It** is a lovely city.
4. **"They"** can refer to people whose gender is unknown or non-binary (modern usage).
   - Alex called. **They** want to meet tomorrow.
5. The subject pronoun always goes **before the verb**.

---

## Positive Examples
```

I love learning English. You speak very clearly. He works at a hospital. She reads every night before bed. It costs five dollars. We travel every summer. You (all) are fantastic students. They live in a big apartment.

```

---

## Negative Examples
```

I don't like cold weather. You aren't listening to me. He doesn't work on weekends. She isn't in the office today. It isn't working properly. We don't have class tomorrow. They aren't my cousins.

```

---

## Questions
```

Do I need to bring anything? Are you free this evening? Is he your coworker? Does she speak French? Is it far from here? Are we taking the bus? Are they coming to the party?

```

---

## Common Mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Me am tired. | **I** am tired. | "Me" is an object pronoun, not subject |
| Him is my boss. | **He** is my boss. | Use subject form before the verb |
| Her is a designer. | **She** is a designer. | Same rule — subject pronoun needed |
| i am happy. | **I** am happy. | "I" is always capitalized |
| The cat, it eats fish. (redundant) | **It** eats fish. | Don't repeat the noun AND pronoun in simple sentences |

---

## Pronunciation

| Pronoun | Pronunciation |
|---------|--------------|
| I | /aɪ/ — "eye" |
| You | /juː/ — "yoo" |
| He | /hiː/ — "hee" |
| She | /ʃiː/ — "shee" |
| It | /ɪt/ — "it" |
| We | /wiː/ — "wee" |
| They | /ðeɪ/ — "thay" |

> **Note:** In fast speech, "he", "she", "we", "they" are often reduced and blended with the following word.
> "He is" → sounds like **"heez"**
> "She is" → sounds like **"sheez"**

---

## Daily Sentences
```

I need a coffee right now. You always know what to say. He called me this morning. She just got promoted at work. It's getting dark outside. We should leave in ten minutes. They moved to a new city last year. I think you're going to love this. It happens to everyone sometimes. We're doing our best.

```

---

## Vocabulary

| Word | Type | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| everyone | pronoun | Everyone is invited. |
| someone | pronoun | Someone left their bag here. |
| nobody | pronoun | Nobody knows the answer. |
| they (neutral) | pronoun | They prefer not to say. |
| both | determiner | Both are correct. |
| all | determiner | We all agree. |

---

## Exercises

### Exercise 1 — Replace the subject with a pronoun

1. **Maria** is a great student. → ___ is a great student.
2. **Carlos and I** are going to the gym. → ___ are going to the gym.
3. **The laptop** is very slow. → ___ is very slow.
4. **John** works from home. → ___ works from home.
5. **My parents** live in Arequipa. → ___ live in Arequipa.
6. **The cat** is sleeping. → ___ is sleeping.
7. **Laura** and **Sofia** are sisters. → ___ are sisters.

**Answers:** 1-She / 2-We / 3-It / 4-He / 5-They / 6-It / 7-They

---

### Exercise 2 — Choose the correct pronoun

1. ___ am learning guitar. (I / Me / My)
2. ___ is a famous scientist. (Her / She / Hers)
3. ___ are from New Zealand. (Them / Their / They)
4. ___ is my favorite movie. (It / Its / He)
5. ___ and I are partners. (Him / He / His)

**Answers:** 1-I / 2-She / 3-They / 4-It / 5-He

---

## Tips

> 💡 **Tip 1:** Always check if the pronoun is the **subject** (doing the action) or the **object** (receiving the action). Subject = I, he, she, we, they. Object = me, him, her, us, them.

> 💡 **Tip 2:** "It" is very flexible in English. Use it for time (It is 3pm), weather (It is sunny), distance (It is far), and things.

> 💡 **Tip 3:** English does not distinguish formal/informal "you" like Spanish (tú/usted). "You" is always correct in any situation.

# 3. Possessive Adjectives

---

## Definition

Possessive adjectives show **who something belongs to**.
They always go **before a noun** and never change form based on the noun — only based on the **owner**.

> "This is **my** book." — "my" tells you the book belongs to me.

---

## Structure

| Subject Pronoun | Possessive Adjective | Example |
|-----------------|---------------------|---------|
| I | **my** | This is **my** phone. |
| You | **your** | Is this **your** bag? |
| He | **his** | That is **his** car. |
| She | **her** | She loves **her** job. |
| It | **its** | The dog wagged **its** tail. |
| We | **our** | We painted **our** house. |
| They | **their** | They forgot **their** keys. |

---

## Rules

1. Possessive adjectives always come **before a noun**.
   - ✅ **My** sister is a doctor. ❌ Sister **my** is a doctor.
2. They agree with the **owner**, not the thing owned.
   - He loves **his** sister. / He loves **his** car. (not "her car" just because "car" is feminine in other languages)
3. **Its** (possessive) ≠ **It's** (it is). This is one of the most common mistakes in English.
4. **Their** ≠ **There** ≠ **They're** — three different words, same sound (homophones).

---

## Positive Examples
```

My name is Andrea. Your English is improving a lot. His favorite sport is football. Her dress is absolutely beautiful. Its engine makes a strange noise. Our teacher is very patient. Their apartment has an amazing view. I love my morning routine. Can I borrow your pen? He misses his hometown.

```

---

## Negative Examples
```

My brother isn't very tall. Your idea isn't bad at all. His schedule isn't flexible. Her answer isn't correct. Our flight isn't on time. Their children aren't old enough.

```

---

## Questions
```

What's your name? Is this your umbrella? What's his job? How old is her daughter? What's our plan for tomorrow? Are their parents coming to visit? Where's my wallet?

```

---

## Common Mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Is this you bag? | Is this **your** bag? | "you" is a pronoun, not possessive |
| Her name's Maria. (referring to a male) | **His** name's Carlos. | Must match the gender of the owner |
| The cat hurt it's paw. | The cat hurt **its** paw. | its = possessive / it's = it is |
| There car is new. | **Their** car is new. | there = place / their = possessive |
| My bag = mine bag | **My** bag / **Mine** | Don't use "mine/yours/his" before a noun |

---

## Pronunciation

| Adjective | Pronunciation |
|-----------|--------------|
| my | /maɪ/ — "my" |
| your | /jɔːr/ — "yor" |
| his | /hɪz/ — "hiz" |
| her | /hɜːr/ — "hur" |
| its | /ɪts/ — "its" |
| our | /aʊər/ — "ow-er" (often sounds like "ar") |
| their | /ðeər/ — "thair" |

---

## Daily Sentences
```

My coffee is getting cold. What's your favorite TV show? His birthday is next Friday. Her parents live abroad. The company changed its logo. Our meeting starts in five minutes. They forgot their tickets at home. Can you give me your number? I lost my keys again. Their wedding was incredible.

```

---

## Vocabulary

| Noun | With possessive | Example |
|------|----------------|---------|
| name | my name | My name is Luis. |
| family | our family | Our family is big. |
| job | her job | Her job is stressful. |
| house | their house | Their house is beautiful. |
| dream | my dream | My dream is to travel the world. |
| opinion | your opinion | Your opinion matters. |
| idea | his idea | His idea was brilliant. |
| phone | my phone | My phone is out of battery. |

---

## Exercises

### Exercise 1 — Fill in the correct possessive adjective

1. I have a dog. ___ dog is called Max.
2. She has a brother. ___ brother is a pilot.
3. They have a car. ___ car is blue.
4. You have a great idea. ___ idea will work.
5. We have a new teacher. ___ teacher is from Canada.
6. He has a bicycle. ___ bicycle is very old.
7. The cat has a toy. ___ toy is a small mouse.

**Answers:** 1-My / 2-Her / 3-Their / 4-Your / 5-Our / 6-His / 7-Its

---

1. Is this you bag? → ___
2. She forgot her keys. (talking about Carlos) → ___
3. The dog hurt it's leg. → ___
4. There house is on this street. → ___

1. Is this **your** bag?
2. He forgot **his** keys.

4. **Their** house is on this street.

---

## Tips

> 💡 **Tip 1:** Remember the owner, not the object. In Spanish "su" covers many cases — in English you must choose between his/her/its/their based on who owns it.

> 💡 **Tip 2:** Memorize this sentence: **"It's raining and the dog hurt its paw."** It has both "it's" and "its" — perfect to remember the difference.

> 💡 **Tip 3:** There / Their / They're — make a card for these three. They sound exactly the same but mean completely different things.

# 4. Numbers

---

## Definition

Numbers in English are used to count, order, measure, give dates, times, phone numbers, prices, and much more. There are two main types:
- **Cardinal numbers** → used for counting (one, two, three…)
- **Ordinal numbers** → used for order and dates (first, second, third…)

---

## Structure

### Cardinal Numbers

| Number | Word | Number | Word |
|--------|------|--------|------|
| 0 | zero | 20 | twenty |
| 1 | one | 21 | twenty-one |
| 2 | two | 30 | thirty |
| 3 | three | 40 | forty |
| 4 | four | 50 | fifty |
| 5 | five | 60 | sixty |
| 6 | six | 70 | seventy |
| 7 | seven | 80 | eighty |
| 8 | eight | 90 | ninety |
| 9 | nine | 100 | one hundred |
| 10 | ten | 1,000 | one thousand |
| 11 | eleven | 10,000 | ten thousand |
| 12 | twelve | 100,000 | one hundred thousand |
| 13 | thirteen | 1,000,000 | one million |
| 14 | fourteen | | |
| 15 | fifteen | | |
| 16 | sixteen | | |
| 17 | seventeen | | |
| 18 | eighteen | | |
| 19 | nineteen | | |

---

### Ordinal Numbers

| Number | Ordinal | Abbreviation |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| 1 | first | 1st |
| 2 | second | 2nd |
| 3 | third | 3rd |
| 4 | fourth | 4th |
| 5 | fifth | 5th |
| 6 | sixth | 6th |
| 7 | seventh | 7th |
| 8 | eighth | 8th |
| 9 | ninth | 9th |
| 10 | tenth | 10th |
| 11 | eleventh | 11th |
| 12 | twelfth | 12th |
| 20 | twentieth | 20th |
| 21 | twenty-first | 21st |
| 30 | thirtieth | 30th |
| 100 | hundredth | 100th |

> **Rule:** Most ordinals = number + **th**. Exceptions: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 5th, 8th, 9th, 12th.

---

## Rules

1. Numbers **21–99** use a hyphen: twenty**-**one, forty**-**five, ninety**-**nine.
2. **"Forty"** — NOT "fourty". Common spelling mistake.
3. **"Hundred"** and **"thousand"** do not take an **-s** after a specific number.
   - ✅ five **hundred** / two **thousand** ❌ five hundreds / two thousands
4. Use **"and"** after hundreds in British English:
   - 245 = two hundred **and** forty-five (British) / two hundred forty-five (American)
5. Ordinal numbers are used for **dates**: My birthday is on the **fifth** of May.
6. **Zero** can also be said as **"oh"** in phone numbers, room numbers, and codes.
   - Phone: 555-0-178 = "five five five **oh** one seven eight"

---

## Positive Examples
```

There are twenty-five students in my class. My apartment is on the seventh floor. The ticket costs thirty-eight dollars. This is my first time in London. She finished second in the competition. He has three brothers and one sister. I need one hundred pages for my report. The meeting is on the third of June.

```

---

## Common Mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| fourty | **forty** | No "u" in forty |
| five hundreds | five **hundred** | No plural after a number |
| the 3 of May | the **third** of May | Use ordinal for dates |
| one thousand and five hundred | **one thousand five hundred** / **fifteen hundred** | "and" only before tens/units |
| I am on the 5 place | I am in **fifth** place | Use ordinal for positions |

---

## Pronunciation

| Number | Tricky Pronunciation |
|--------|---------------------|
| 13 | thirTEEN (stress on last syllable) |
| 30 | THIRty (stress on first syllable) |
| 15 | fifTEEN |
| 50 | FIFty |
| 18 | eighTEEN |
| 80 | EIGHty |
| 1,000,000 | one MILLion |

> **Tip:** Numbers ending in **-teen** (13-19) are stressed on the LAST syllable.
> Numbers ending in **-ty** (30-90) are stressed on the FIRST syllable.

---

## Daily Sentences
```

Can I have two coffees, please? My phone number is 987-654-321. She lives on the fourth floor. The store is open until nine o'clock. There are thirty days in September. This book has over three hundred pages. I wake up at six thirty every morning. Today is the twenty-second of March. We need twelve eggs for the recipe. He scored ninety-five on the exam.

```

---

## Exercises

### Exercise 1 — Write the number in words

1. 15 → ___
2. 47 → ___
3. 200 → ___
4. 1,500 → ___
5. 83 → ___

**Answers:** 1-fifteen / 2-forty-seven / 3-two hundred / 4-one thousand five hundred / 5-eighty-three

### Exercise 2 — Write the ordinal

1. 1 → ___
2. 3 → ___
3. 12 → ___
4. 22 → ___
5. 50 → ___

**Answers:** 1-first / 2-third / 3-twelfth / 4-twenty-second / 5-fiftieth

---

## Tips

> 💡 **Tip 1:** Practice numbers by saying your phone number, address, and age out loud in English every day.

> 💡 **Tip 2:** Remember: **13 vs 30, 14 vs 40, 15 vs 50**. The difference is the stress. Thirteen (stress: teen) vs Thirty (stress: thir).

> 💡 **Tip 3:** Forty has NO "u". Repeat: **for-ty**, not **four-ty**.

# 5. Days and Months

---

## Definition

The **days of the week** and **months of the year** are essential vocabulary for talking about time, making plans, and describing schedules. In English, **all days and months are always capitalized**.

---

## Structure

### Days of the Week

| Day | Abbreviation | Pronunciation |
|-----|-------------|--------------|
| **Monday** | Mon | /ˈmʌndeɪ/ |
| **Tuesday** | Tue | /ˈtjuːzdeɪ/ |
| **Wednesday** | Wed | /ˈwenzdeɪ/ |
| **Thursday** | Thu | /ˈθɜːrzdeɪ/ |
| **Friday** | Fri | /ˈfraɪdeɪ/ |
| **Saturday** | Sat | /ˈsætərdeɪ/ |
| **Sunday** | Sun | /ˈsʌndeɪ/ |

> The week in English-speaking countries often **starts on Sunday** (especially in the US). In most of the world, it starts on **Monday**.

---

### Prepositions with Days and Months

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|-------------|-----|---------|
| **on** | specific days and dates | **on** Monday / **on** the 5th of July |
| **in** | months, years, seasons | **in** January / **in** 2024 / **in** summer |
| **at** | specific times | **at** 9 o'clock / **at** noon / **at** night |
| **last** | past reference | **last** Friday / **last** month |
| **next** | future reference | **next** Tuesday / **next** year |
| **every** | recurring | **every** Monday / **every** December |
| **this** | current week/month | **this** Saturday / **this** November |

---

## Rules

1. Days and months are **always capitalized** in English.
   - ✅ I love **Friday**. ❌ I love friday.
2. Use **"on"** with days: on Monday, on Saturday morning.
3. Use **"in"** with months: in March, in December.
4. Use **"the"** + ordinal + **"of"** for full dates:
   - The **fifth** of **April** / **April fifth** (American style)
5. **Wednesday** is pronounced "WENZ-day" — the silent **d** in the middle is not spoken.
6. **February** is often pronounced "FEB-yoo-ery" in fast speech (the first "r" disappears).

## Common Mistakes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| I go on mondays to the gym. | I go to the gym **on Mondays**. | Always capitalize / word order |
| My birthday is in 5 of March. | My birthday is **on the fifth of March**. | Use "on" + ordinal for dates |
| See you at Friday. | See you **on** Friday. | "on" for days, not "at" |
| I was born in monday. | I was born **on** a Monday. | "on" for days |
| february | **February** | Always capitalize months |
| Wendsday | **Wednesday** | Common spelling error |

## Tips

> 💡 **Tip 1:** Always capitalize days and months in English — unlike Spanish, where "lunes" and "enero" are lowercase.

> 💡 **Tip 2:** Remember the preposition triangle: **ON** (days & dates) / **IN** (months, years, seasons) / **AT** (specific times).

> 💡 **Tip 3:** A helpful rhyme to remember month lengths: *"30 days hath September, April, June and November. All the rest have 31, except February."*

> 💡 **Tip 4:** Wednesday = WENZ-day. Practice it until it feels natural — most learners mispronounce it.
