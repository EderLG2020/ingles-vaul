## 🔷 1. WH- Questions

[[WH Questions]] are **open questions** — they ask for specific information, not just "yes" or "no."

### Grammar Structure

```
WH- word + Auxiliary Verb + Subject + Main Verb + (Complement)?
```

|Part|Example|
|---|---|
|WH- word|_What, Who, Where, When…_|
|Auxiliary|_do, does, did, is, are…_|
|Subject|_you, he, they…_|
|Main Verb|_eat, go, study…_|

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** En español las preguntas no siempre cambian el orden de las palabras (_¿Tú vas al cine?_). En inglés, el auxiliar SIEMPRE va antes del sujeto: _Where **do** you go?_ — no _Where you go?_

---

### [[What]]

Used to ask about **things, information, or actions**.

|Structure|Example|
|---|---|
|What + aux + subject + verb?|_What do you eat for breakfast?_|
|What + be + subject?|_What is your name?_|
|What + noun + aux + subject + verb?|_What time do you wake up?_|

**Examples:**

- **Formal:** _What is the purpose of this meeting?_
- **Informal:** _What are you doing tonight?_
- **A1:** _What is this?_
- **B2:** _What would you have done differently?_

> [!TIP] **What** vs **Which** → Use **What** when choices are unlimited. Use **Which** when choices are limited.
> 
> - _What color do you like?_ (any color)
> - _Which color do you prefer, red or blue?_ (limited options)

---

### [[Who]]

Used to ask about **people** (subject or object).

|Role|Structure|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Subject|Who + verb?|_Who called you?_|
|Object|Who + aux + subject + verb?|_Who did you call?_|

**Examples:**

- _Who is your teacher?_
- _Who did you meet at the party?_
- **Formal:** _To whom should I address this letter?_
- **Informal:** _Who are you texting?_

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** _Who_ puede ser sujeto o complemento. Cuando es sujeto, **no se usa auxiliar**:
> 
> - _Who **called**?_ → ¿Quién llamó? ✅ (sin _did_)
> - _Who did you **call**?_ → ¿A quién llamaste? ✅ (con _did_)

---

### [[Where]]

Used to ask about **place or location**.

```
Where + auxiliary + subject + verb?
```

**Examples:**

- _Where do you live?_
- _Where is the nearest hospital?_
- **Formal:** _Where was the conference held?_
- **Informal:** _Where are you?_
- **B2:** _Where would you go if you could travel anywhere?_

---

### [[When]]

Used to ask about **time**.

```
When + auxiliary + subject + verb?
```

**Examples:**

- _When does the movie start?_
- _When did you arrive?_
- **Formal:** _When is the deadline for submission?_
- **Informal:** _When are you free?_

> [!TIP] **When** vs **What time** → Both ask about time, but _what time_ is more specific.
> 
> - _When do you wake up?_ → General (morning, early, late)
> - _What time do you wake up?_ → Specific (7:00 a.m.)

---

### [[Why]]

Used to ask about **reasons or causes**.

```
Why + auxiliary + subject + verb?
```

**Examples:**

- _Why are you laughing?_
- _Why did she leave early?_
- **Formal:** _Why was the project delayed?_
- **Informal:** _Why didn't you tell me?_

**Common Answer Patterns:**

|Question|Answer|
|---|---|
|_Why are you late?_|_Because the bus was delayed._|
|_Why do you study English?_|_To get a better job._ / _For my career._|

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** La respuesta a _Why_ usa **because** (porque), **to + infinitive** (para), o **for + noun** (por). No se responde con _why not_ de forma afirmativa como en español.

---

### [[How]]

Used to ask about **manner, condition, or degree**.

|Combination|Meaning|Example|
|---|---|---|
|How|Manner / Condition|_How are you?_|
|How + adjective|Degree|_How tall is he?_|
|How + adverb|Frequency/Degree|_How often do you exercise?_|
|How long|Duration|_How long does it take?_|
|How far|Distance|_How far is the station?_|
|How old|Age|_How old are you?_|
|How much|Quantity (uncountable)|_How much water do you drink?_|
|How many|Quantity (countable)|_How many books do you have?_|

**Examples:**

- _How do you spell your name?_
- _How long have you been waiting?_
- **Formal:** _How would you describe your management style?_
- **Informal:** _How's it going?_

---

### [[Which]]

Used to ask about **choices from a limited set**.

```
Which + (noun) + auxiliary + subject + verb?
```

**Examples:**

- _Which bus goes to the city center?_
- _Which of these options do you prefer?_
- **Formal:** _Which department handles customer complaints?_
- **Informal:** _Which one is yours?_

> [!WARNING] ⚠️ **Common Mistake:** Using _What_ instead of _Which_ when options are specific.
> 
> - ❌ _What book did you choose, Harry Potter or Narnia?_
> - ✅ _Which book did you choose, Harry Potter or Narnia?_

---

### [[Whose]]

Used to ask about **possession or ownership**.

```
Whose + noun + auxiliary + subject + verb?
```

**Examples:**

- _Whose bag is this?_
- _Whose idea was it?_
- **Formal:** _Whose signature is required on this document?_
- **Informal:** _Whose turn is it?_

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** _Whose_ = ¿De quién…? No confundir con _Who's_ (= Who is).
> 
> - _Whose book is this?_ → ¿De quién es este libro?
> - _Who's there?_ → ¿Quién está ahí?

---

### [[How Much vs How Many]]

|Feature|How Much|How Many|
|---|---|---|
|Used with|Uncountable nouns|Countable nouns|
|Examples (nouns)|water, money, time, sugar|books, students, cars, hours|
|Question|_How much milk do you need?_|_How many eggs do you have?_|
|Answer|_A little / A lot / 500ml_|_A few / Ten / None_|

**Practice Comparison:**

|Noun|How Much or How Many?|
|---|---|
|sugar|How **much** sugar?|
|apples|How **many** apples?|
|information|How **much** information?|
|people|How **many** people?|
|homework|How **much** homework?|

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** _How much_ = ¿Cuánto/a? | _How many_ = ¿Cuántos/as? La diferencia sigue la misma lógica que en español: nombres contables vs. incontables.

## 🔶 2. Yes/No Questions

[[Yes No Questions]] require only **Yes** or **No** as an answer.

### Grammar Structure

```
Auxiliary Verb + Subject + Main Verb + (Complement)?
```

### Auxiliary Verbs by Tense

|Tense|Auxiliary|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Present Simple|do / does|_Do you like coffee?_|
|Past Simple|did|_Did she call you?_|
|Present Continuous|am / is / are|_Are they coming?_|
|Past Continuous|was / were|_Were you sleeping?_|
|Present Perfect|have / has|_Have you eaten?_|
|Future (will)|will|_Will you help me?_|
|Modals|can, could, should…|_Can you swim?_|

**Examples:**

- _Do you speak English?_ → _Yes, I do. / No, I don't._
- _Is she your sister?_ → _Yes, she is. / No, she isn't._
- **Formal:** _Would you be available for a meeting on Friday?_
- **Informal:** _Did you see that?_

> [!TIP] **Short Answers** always repeat the auxiliary — never the main verb.
> 
> - ✅ _Do you like pizza?_ → _Yes, I **do**._
> - ❌ _Yes, I like._

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** En español se puede preguntar con entonación: _¿Hablas inglés?_ En inglés es obligatorio invertir el orden: _Do you speak English?_ — no _You speak English?_ (solo en lenguaje muy informal o coloquial).

## 🔹 3. Tag Questions

[[Tag Questions]] are short questions added to the end of a statement to **confirm information** or **invite agreement**.

### Grammar Structure

```
Positive statement + negative tag?
Negative statement + positive tag?
```

### Formation Rules

|Rule|Example|
|---|---|
|Positive + negative tag|_It's cold today, **isn't it**?_|
|Negative + positive tag|_You don't smoke, **do you**?_|
|With _I am_|_I'm late, **aren't I**?_|
|With modal verbs|_She can drive, **can't she**?_|
|With _used to_|_He used to live here, **didn't he**?_|
|With _have got_|_They've got a car, **haven't they**?_|

### Complete Conjugation Table

|Statement|Tag|
|---|---|
|_She is a doctor,_|_isn't she?_|
|_They are coming,_|_aren't they?_|
|_He was there,_|_wasn't he?_|
|_You were tired,_|_weren't you?_|
|_You speak French,_|_don't you?_|
|_He likes coffee,_|_doesn't he?_|
|_They went home,_|_didn't they?_|
|_She can swim,_|_can't she?_|
|_You will come,_|_won't you?_|
|_They have finished,_|_haven't they?_|

**Intonation matters:**

|Intonation|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Falling ↘|Speaker is sure, seeking agreement|
|Rising ↗|Speaker is genuinely unsure|

**Examples:**

- _It's a beautiful day, isn't it?_ ↘ (I think so, agree with me)
- _You've been here before, haven't you?_ ↗ (I'm not sure, tell me)
- **Formal:** _The report was submitted on time, wasn't it?_
- **Informal:** _That was great, wasn't it?_

> [!WARNING] ⚠️ **Common Mistake:** Using the same polarity in both parts.
> 
> - ❌ _It's cold, **is it**?_
> - ✅ _It's cold, **isn't it**?_

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** El equivalente en español es _¿verdad?, ¿no?, ¿cierto?_ — siempre igual. En inglés, el _tag_ cambia según el auxiliar y la polaridad de la oración principal.

## 🔸 4. Indirect Questions

[[Indirect Questions]] are **polite, embedded questions** within another sentence. They are softer and more formal than direct questions.

### Grammar Structure

```
Introductory phrase + question word + subject + verb (normal word order)
```

> [!IMPORTANT] In indirect questions, **word order returns to normal** (no inversion of subject and auxiliary).

### Introductory Phrases

|Type|Phrase|
|---|---|
|Polite request|_Could you tell me…_|
|Polite request|_Would you mind telling me…_|
|Wonder|_I wonder…_|
|Uncertainty|_I'm not sure…_|
|Want to know|_I'd like to know…_|
|Ask|_Can you tell me…_|

### Direct → Indirect Comparison

|Direct Question|Indirect Question|
|---|---|
|_Where is the bank?_|_Could you tell me **where the bank is**?_|
|_What time does it close?_|_Do you know **what time it closes**?_|
|_Why did she leave?_|_I wonder **why she left**._|
|_Is the meeting cancelled?_|_Could you tell me **if the meeting is cancelled**?_|
|_Does he speak English?_|_I'd like to know **whether he speaks English**._|

> [!NOTE] For **Yes/No** indirect questions, use **if** or **whether** instead of a WH- word:
> 
> - _Do you know **if** there's a pharmacy nearby?_
> - _I'm not sure **whether** she's coming._

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** Las preguntas indirectas en inglés NO invierten el orden sujeto-verbo. Es el mismo error que cometen los hispanohablantes: _Can you tell me where **is** the station?_ ❌ → _Can you tell me where **the station is**?_ ✅

## 🔺 5. Auxiliary Inversion

[[Auxiliary Inversion]] is the grammatical mechanism that **creates questions in English** by placing the auxiliary verb before the subject.

### How It Works

```
Statement:  Subject + Auxiliary + Main Verb
Question:   Auxiliary + Subject + Main Verb
```

|Statement|Question|
|---|---|
|_She **is** studying._|_**Is** she studying?_|
|_They **have** finished._|_**Have** they finished?_|
|_He **can** swim._|_**Can** he swim?_|
|_You **will** come._|_**Will** you come?_|

### The DO / DOES / DID Rule

When there is **no auxiliary verb** in the statement, use **do / does / did**:

|Tense|Statement|Question|
|---|---|---|
|Present Simple (I/You/We/They)|_You like jazz._|_**Do** you like jazz?_|
|Present Simple (He/She/It)|_She works late._|_**Does** she work late?_|
|Past Simple|_They went home._|_**Did** they go home?_|

> [!WARNING] ⚠️ **Common Mistake:** Using the main verb in its original form after _do/does/did_.
> 
> - ❌ _Does she **works** here?_
> - ✅ _Does she **work** here?_

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** El verbo _do_ como auxiliar de pregunta no tiene equivalente en español. _Do you like music?_ = _¿Te gusta la música?_ — el _do_ no se traduce; simplemente invierte el orden y forma la pregunta.

## 🔻 6. Subject Questions

[[Subject Questions]] ask about the **subject** of the sentence — _who_ or _what_ performed the action.

### Key Rule

> In subject questions, **there is NO auxiliary verb** and the word order does **NOT** invert.

### Comparison: Subject vs. Object Questions

|Type|Question|Who/What is being asked about?|
|---|---|---|
|**Subject**|_Who **called** you?_|The caller (subject)|
|**Object**|_Who did you **call**?_|The person called (object)|
|**Subject**|_What **happened**?_|The event (subject)|
|**Object**|_What did you **do**?_|The action performed (object)|

### More Examples

|Subject Question|Object Question|
|---|---|
|_Who **lives** here?_|_Who do you **live** with?_|
|_What **caused** the problem?_|_What did you **cause**?_|
|_Which team **won**?_|_Which team did you **support**?_|
|_How many students **passed**?_|_How many students did the teacher **pass**?_|

### Structure Comparison Table

```
Subject Q:  Who    + verb (+ object)?
            Who    called   you?

Object Q:   Who    + auxiliary + subject + verb?
            Who    did        you       call?
```

> [!TIP] **Quick Test:** If you can replace the WH- word with a subject pronoun (_he, she, they_) and the sentence still makes sense without changing anything else — it's a **subject question**.
> 
> - _Who called?_ → _He called._ ✅ → Subject question
> - _Who did you call?_ → _He did you call?_ ❌ → Object question

> [!NOTE] 🇪🇸 **Para hispanohablantes:** Esto es similar al español. _¿Quién llamó?_ (sujeto) vs. _¿A quién llamaste?_ (objeto). La diferencia clave en inglés es que en la pregunta de sujeto, **no se usa _did_**.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

[[Common Mistakes]] section to review the most frequent errors learners make.

### Top 10 Errors

|#|Error|Incorrect ❌|Correct ✅|
|---|---|---|---|
|1|Missing auxiliary in WH- questions|_Where you live?_|_Where do you live?_|
|2|Wrong auxiliary after _does_|_Does she works?_|_Does she work?_|
|3|Inversion in indirect questions|_Can you tell me where is he?_|_Can you tell me where he is?_|
|4|Using auxiliary in subject questions|_Who did call you?_|_Who called you?_|
|5|Wrong tag polarity|_She's nice, is she?_|_She's nice, isn't she?_|
|6|Confusing _Whose_ and _Who's_|_Whose coming?_|_Who's coming?_|
|7|Using _How much_ with countable nouns|_How much books?_|_How many books?_|
|8|Answering with full verb in short answers|_Yes, I like._|_Yes, I do._|
|9|Wrong word order in Yes/No questions|_You are ready?_|_Are you ready?_|
|10|Using _What_ instead of _Which_|_What one do you prefer?_|_Which one do you prefer?_|

## 🔄 Comparisons Between Similar Question Types

### Direct vs. Indirect

|Feature|Direct|Indirect|
|---|---|---|
|Formality|Neutral / Informal|Formal / Polite|
|Word order|Inverted|Normal|
|Example|_Where is the station?_|_Could you tell me where the station is?_|

### Subject vs. Object Questions

|Feature|Subject|Object|
|---|---|---|
|Asks about|Who/What does the action|Who/What receives the action|
|Auxiliary|Not used|Used|
|Example|_Who helped you?_|_Who did you help?_|

### Tag Questions: Falling vs. Rising Intonation

|Intonation|Purpose|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Falling ↘|Confirmation, rhetorical|_You're a doctor, aren't you?_ ↘|
|Rising ↗|Genuine question|_She passed, didn't she?_ ↗|

### What vs. Which vs. Whose

|Word|Use|Example|
|---|---|---|
|What|Open choice / things / actions|_What do you do?_|
|Which|Limited choice|_Which door should I open?_|
|Whose|Possession|_Whose pen is this?_|

### Exercise 5 — Error Correction (B1–B2)

Find and correct the mistake in each question.

```
1. Where you are going?
2. Does she speaks Italian?
3. Can you tell me where is the exit?
4. Who did wrote this report?
5. How much children do you have?
6. She's your friend, is she?
```

<details> <summary>✅ Answer Key</summary>

1. _Where **are you** going?_ (auxiliary inversion)
2. _Does she **speak** Italian?_ (base form after does)
3. _Can you tell me where **the exit is**?_ (no inversion in indirect)
4. _**Who wrote** this report?_ (no auxiliary in subject question)
5. _**How many** children do you have?_ (countable noun)
6. _She's your friend, **isn't she**?_ (opposite polarity)

</details>
